## Section 1 — The 60 Most-Asked Questions (Ranked)

Ranked by combined Google search volume + TikTok comment frequency + Reddit community patterns.

**1. Does botox make your wrinkles worse when it wears off?**
- Treatment: Botox · Why viral: #1 fear holding first-timers back
- Secret answer: No — wrinkles return to baseline. A landmark twin study showed long-term botox users had measurably fewer wrinkles 13 years later. The "it makes you look worse" belief is clinically false.

**2. How much is botox?**
- Treatment: Botox · Why viral: #1 Google PAA globally
- Secret answer: Most clinics price by "area" not units — that's how they hide what you're getting. Ask for unit price. Average: €10–€20/unit. You're often paying for the injector's postcode, not the product.

**3. Does lip filler migrate?**
- Treatment: Lip Filler · Why viral: 30M+ TikTok views, fear-driven loop
- Secret answer: It can — but it's rarer than TikTok makes it look. Real cause isn't the filler, it's the injector. High volume, wrong placement, wrong product viscosity.

**4. What age should you start botox?**
- Treatment: Botox preventative · Why viral: 46.6M TikTok baby botox views, NPR Nov 2025
- Secret answer: The "right age" question is wrong. What's your muscle activity? Some 22-year-olds need it earlier than some 35-year-olds. Your face, not your birthday.

**5. What is the lip flip — is it better than lip filler?**
- Treatment: Botox lip flip · Why viral: #lipflip 162M views, fail videos millions
- Secret answer: The lip flip can stop you drinking through a straw or saying the letter P. Lasts 2–3 months. For most people, a tiny amount of filler gives better, longer, more predictable results.

**6. Can you get botox while breastfeeding?**
- Treatment: Botox · Why viral: massive underserved audience
- Secret answer: No evidence of harm, but zero safety studies on breastfeeding women. Responsible answer: wait.

**7. Why does botox sometimes look frozen?**
- Treatment: Botox · Why viral: #1 booking barrier
- Secret answer: Frozen = too many units + wrong muscle mapping. The dose perfect for one looks alien on another. Technique problem, not product problem.

**8. Is salmon DNA the same as salmon sperm?**
- Treatment: Polynucleotides · Why viral: "sperm" angle = shock value = shares
- Secret answer: Technically yes — comes from purified salmon sperm DNA. But you're not getting sperm injected. The viral name wins because it sounds disgusting and people cannot look away.

**9. Does filler under the eyes make bags worse?**
- Treatment: Tear trough filler · Why viral: most-searched "filler disaster"
- Secret answer: With true fat herniation (structural bags), yes — filler adds volume where there's already too much. Tyndall effect (blue-grey tinge) affects 1 in 5 patients with inexperienced injectors.

**10. How long does masseter botox last?**
- Treatment: Masseter Botox · Why viral: top comment under every masseter video
- Secret answer: 4–6 months relaxation. 3–6 months visible slimming. Stop and the muscle comes back. Jaw slimming is a rental, not a purchase.

**11. Can filler make you look older?**
- Treatment: Filler all types · Why viral: 2025 "filler fatigue" celeb trend
- Secret answer: Yes. Over-volumised cheeks create downward pull and eventually jowling. Kylie Jenner dissolving jaw filler went viral because she looked younger without it.

**12. Does botox prevent wrinkles if you start young?**
- Treatment: Botox preventative · Why viral: NPR, BBC late 2025 coverage
- Secret answer: Twin study evidence says yes. But you need to start before static lines form. Once the crease is there at rest, botox treats it but can't erase the memory.

**13. What is Profhilo and how is it different from filler?**
- Treatment: Profhilo · Why viral: top-of-funnel unfamiliar treatment
- Secret answer: Profhilo isn't filler. It doesn't add volume. It spreads through tissue and tells skin cells to make collagen. A gym membership for your skin.

**14. What does masseter botox do to your face shape?**
- Treatment: Masseter Botox · Why viral: visible transformation goes viral
- Secret answer: Slims lower third, makes face more heart-shaped. Side effect: it can cause jowling. Slimming the muscle removes structural support for the skin above.

**15. Does dissolving filler hurt?**
- Treatment: Hyaluronidase · Why viral: rising "dissolve everything" trend
- Secret answer: Stings more than original filler. Not precise — dissolves everything including your natural HA. You look deflated for 2–4 weeks.

**16. What is Sculptra and why is it so expensive?**
- Treatment: Sculptra · Why viral: high price = curiosity tension
- Secret answer: Contains no filler. Poly-L-lactic acid microspheres trigger controlled inflammation. Your body builds collagen around them. Cost = collagen you grow yourself.

**17. Can lip filler look natural?**
- Treatment: Lip Filler · Why viral: "natural" is #1 search modifier
- Secret answer: Yes — when ratio is right. Phi ratio (upper:lower = 1:1.6) gives the most natural result. Duck lips come from Russian technique on people who weren't candidates.

**18. What is "Ozempic face" and can filler fix it?**
- Treatment: Filler + Sculptra · Why viral: GLP-1 drugs mainstream
- Secret answer: Rapid fat loss isn't selective — face loses too. Filler adds volume. Sculptra tightens. Together they rebuild structure without puffiness.

**19. Can fat dissolving injections go wrong?**
- Treatment: Lemon Bottle / Aqualyx · Why viral: 81.5M TikTok views + Swissmedic ban
- Secret answer: Yes, badly. Lemon Bottle was unregulated. Complications include abscesses and necrosis. Zero human clinical trials for its main mechanism.

**20. What is baby botox and does it actually work?**
- Treatment: Botox · Why viral: 46.6M views, NPR Nov 2025
- Secret answer: Works as prevention. "Baby botox" = fewer units, not the same units smaller. Some clinics sell full-dose treatment as "baby botox" marketing.

**21. Does masseter botox cause jowls?**
- Treatment: Masseter Botox · Why viral: fear question under every video
- Secret answer: Potentially yes. Heavy repeated dosing causes muscle atrophy and loss of structural support — skin sags.

**22. Profhilo vs polynucleotides — what's the difference?**
- Treatment: Both · Why viral: mechanism confusion
- Secret answer: Profhilo = pure HA, spreads, triggers bioremodelling. Polynucleotides = DNA fragments, direct fibroblast stimulation. Profhilo faster. PN deeper. Together = current gold standard.

**23. How do I know if my injector is good?**
- Treatment: All · Why viral: universal safety concern
- Secret answer: Ask them how they manage a vascular occlusion. If they hesitate, leave. An injector without hyaluronidase in clinic is not safe to see.

**24. What happens if you keep getting filler for years?**
- Treatment: Filler · Why viral: MRI study went viral
- Secret answer: MRI studies show HA filler persisting years longer than advertised. Filler is hydrophilic — attracts water, can expand. 10 years = layers of old product compounding.

**25. Does tear trough filler cause blindness?**
- Treatment: Tear trough · Why viral: 511 documented vision loss cases (AAO 2024)
- Secret answer: It can. Glabella and nose higher risk. Many ethical injectors now refuse tear troughs or only with ultrasound guidance.

**26. What does botox feel like?**
- Treatment: Botox · Why viral: first-timer POV anxiety
- Secret answer: Small ant sting. 10–15 minutes total. Anxiety beforehand is always worse than the procedure.

**27. Can you look natural after years of filler?**
- Treatment: Filler · Why viral: the "I can tell" fear
- Secret answer: Yes — with intentional restraint each session. Incremental drift is the killer. Women who look amazing at 50 with filler have an injector who says no more than yes.

**28. How do polynucleotides work?**
- Treatment: PDRN · Why viral: new treatment, curiosity-driven
- Secret answer: PDRN fragments attach to adenosine receptors, triggering repair cascade — collagen, elastin, HA from inside out. The only injectable that fixes architecture, not fills gaps.

**29. Is it safe to get filler in your 20s?**
- Treatment: Filler · Why viral: age debate + ethics = engagement
- Secret answer: Depends on why. Volume loss at 24 from genetics = legitimate. Filler at 22 from TikTok dysmorphia = not. The injector's job is to say no when patient doesn't need it.

**30. What is Traptox / Barbie Tox?**
- Treatment: Trapezius Botox · Why viral: #Traptox 22.2M views
- Secret answer: Botox in trapezius slims neck/shoulders, elongates the neck. Also relieves tension headaches. Subtle but dramatic in photos.

**31. What happens if I stop getting botox?**
- Treatment: Botox · Why viral: Eloise Anderson 3.7M-view botox-free TikTok
- Secret answer: Muscles wake up, pre-existing wrinkles return — no faster than if you'd never had botox. You won't look worse than baseline.

**32. Does fat dissolving work on double chin?**
- Treatment: Fat dissolving · Why viral: top body concern globally
- Secret answer: Yes — but 2–6 sessions, painful, 2 weeks swelling. Permanent if you maintain weight. Gain weight, fat redistributes.

## Section 3 — The Voice Pattern Guide

**Core characteristics of viral aesthetic doctors:**

- **Sentence length:** Short and punchy in the hook — never more than 12 words in the first 5 seconds. Then vary: 3 short, 1 medium, key reveal. Never uniform throughout.
- **"You" everywhere:** Direct address. "Your face." "You probably think." "You're doing this wrong." Never "patients" in the hook. Always "you."
- **Hot takes, no hedging:** They pick a side. "Don't get lip filler until you're 25." Not "lip filler may be inappropriate for some younger patients." If you need a paragraph of nuance upfront, you've lost them.
- **Storytelling structure:** Specific patient → specific moment → sensory detail → reveal. Not: "Many patients come in wanting change." Instead: "She walked in holding her phone with a screenshot of Bella Hadid's jaw. She was 23. She didn't need anything."
- **Numbers and specifics:** "I've done 600 of these." "This takes 4 minutes." "She came back at 6 weeks and I nearly didn't recognise her." Vague experience claims = invisible. Specific numbers = credible.
- **Mild edge / occasional cussing:** Used sparingly. "This is complete BS." "I'm not doing it. Period." Works when it punctuates an important point — not as general style.
- **What they don't do:** No "indications." No "modality." No "outcomes." No "studies suggest" without naming the study. No "it's important to note." No jargon without immediate plain-English translation. No equivocating.

## Section 4 — The AI-Tell Killlist

Every item below is a signal. One occurrence = flag. Multiple = rewrite.

**Structural tells**
- Em dashes (—) in conversational text
- "It's not just X — it's Y"
- "Not only X, but also Y"
- Pre-summarising before delivering ("In this video I'll explain...")
- Listicle openings in conversational text ("First... Second... Third...")
- Constant rule-of-three lists (three adjectives, three benefits, three reasons)
- Semicolons in conversational content
- Colon-based rhetorical openers ("Here's the thing:", "Think about it:", "There's a catch:")
- Perfectly uniform sentence length throughout
- Zero typos, zero dropped contractions, zero fragments
- Every paragraph ends on an upbeat resolution

**Word-level tells**
- "Delve / dive into / unpack / explore"
- "Navigate / journey / landscape / realm"
- "Game-changer / pivotal / paramount / leverage"
- "Subtle yet powerful / understated yet impactful"
- "Empower / nurture / curate / cultivate"
- "Elevate / transform / revolutionise / reimagine"
- "Holistic / comprehensive / multifaceted"
- "Stark / sobering"
- "Furthermore / Moreover / Indeed / Thus / Hence"
- "Tapestry / fabric / cornerstone / hallmark"
- "Shed light on / bring to light / illuminate"
- "In the realm of / in the world of / in the landscape of"
- "In today's fast-paced world / In today's digital age"
- "Whether you're X or Y"
- "Designed to / crafted to / tailored to" as sentence openers
- "Cutting-edge / state-of-the-art / groundbreaking"
- "A testament to / speaks to / underscores"
- "Robust / seamless / scalable" in non-tech contexts
- Triple adjectives ("warm, confident, empowering")
- "Studies suggest / research shows" without naming the study
- "It's important to note / It's worth mentioning"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Let me tell you / Here's the thing / The truth is" as openers
- "Unwavering commitment / dedicated focus"
- "In conclusion / to summarize / to wrap up"

**Phrase pattern tells**
- "Whether you're looking to X or Y, [brand] has you covered"
- "From X to Y, we've got everything you need"
- "Designed with YOU in mind"
- "Your skin deserves the best"
- "Unlock the secret to..."
- "Say goodbye to X and hello to Y"
- "[Treatment] that is taking [city] by storm"
- "More than just a treatment — it's an experience"
- All-passive voice throughout
- Every exclamation mark feels placed rather than earned
- Zero conflict, zero uncertainty, zero "I don't know"
- Overly inclusive: "no matter your age, skin type, or concern"
- Hyphenated compound adjectives stacked
- "Your journey to [X] starts here"
- "Backed by science, inspired by you"

**Voice tells**
- Talks at the reader instead of to them
- Never admits uncertainty or says "it depends"
- Balanced takes that offend no one and persuade no one
- No personal story, no specific person, no sensory detail
- Generic expertise claims ("our team of experts")
- Closing CTA sounds like a press release
- Uses "journey" for any personal transformation
- Reads fine but sounds unnatural when read aloud
- No sentence starts with "But" or "And"
- No sentence is a fragment
- The piece could have been written about anything, for any brand
