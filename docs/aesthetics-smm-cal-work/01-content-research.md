## 4. Trending Angles RIGHT NOW (2026)

Based on aesthetic-industry knowledge current to early 2026, and the stated context of the brief:

### A. Ozempic Face (HIGH HEAT)
GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide/tirzepatide) have driven mass rapid weight loss. The resulting facial volume deflation — sunken temples, hollow cheeks, accentuated nasolabial folds, deflated under-eyes — has created a new patient cohort: slim body, prematurely aged face. Creators who address this directly ("You lost the weight — now let's restore your face") are getting outsized engagement. Sculptra + conservative HA filler is the go-to protocol narrative. This is highly relevant to the 35–55 Malta demographic.

**Content angle:** "The Ozempic Face Fix — What the Scale Doesn't Tell You"

### B. Polynucleotides (PDRN/PN) for Under-Eyes (RISING)
Post-2024 hesitation around tear trough HA filler (due to widely shared dissolution case studies and post-dissolve controversy on social media), patients are pivoting to PDRN/PN injections which are regenerative, not volumising. This is a legitimate clinical trend: lower risk profile, addresses the root cause (tissue quality) rather than masking it. Major conversation happening on RealSelf, TikTok aesthetics communities, and practitioner forums.

**Content angle:** "Why I'm Recommending Less Filler for Under-Eyes in 2025/26" + "Polynucleotides vs. Tear Trough Filler"

### C. "Tweakment" Culture (SUSTAINED TREND)
The backlash against obvious filler has fully matured into a positive trend: the "no-makeup natural" look achieved with micro-doses. Baby Botox + skin booster + barely-there filler. Women in their 20s and early 30s want to preserve their natural look while optimising skin quality. This language ("tweakments") resonates strongly on TikTok and in UK/European markets which Malta's demographic aligns with.

**Content angle:** "Your Best Skin, Not a Different Face — The Tweakment Philosophy"

### D. Korean Glass-Skin via Injectables (RISING in 2026)
K-beauty's "glass skin" aesthetic — luminous, pore-less, almost translucent skin — has crossed from topical skincare into the injectable world. Skin booster + mesotherapy + Profhilo layering is being marketed as the "injectable glass-skin protocol." Heavy engagement on TikTok, especially among 25–35 demographic. Strong creative opportunity for before/after reels showing texture improvement.

**Content angle:** "The Korean Glass-Skin Injection Protocol We're Doing at Carisma"

### E. Natural Jawline Filler (SHIFT FROM SHARP TO SOFT)
After years of the "snatched" sharp jawline trend, the 2025-2026 shift is toward a softer, anatomically natural jaw definition. Patients are actively asking for "subtle contour, not a square jaw." Practitioners who speak to this nuance — using less product, different injection points — are differentiating themselves.

**Content angle:** "Why I Don't Chase the 'Snatched' Jaw Anymore — And What I Do Instead"

### F. Masseter Botox for Non-Aesthetic Reasons (GROWING AWARENESS)
The medical angle (bruxism, TMJ pain, tension headaches) is getting traction because it justifies the treatment to patients who feel aesthetics is "vain." It's also an entry point for first-time patients who would never book a cosmetic consultation. Strong opportunity for educational content.

**Content angle:** "I've Been Prescribing Masseter Botox for Headaches, Not Just Jaw Slimming"

### G. Under-Eye Filler Hesitation / Dissolving Controversies (ACTIVE CONVERSATION)
Multiple viral social media posts (2023–2025) about complications, Tyndall effect, and the difficulty of dissolving poorly placed tear trough filler have created widespread patient anxiety. Practitioners who address this transparently — explaining why they're cautious, who they will and won't treat, and what alternatives exist — are building significant trust and earning "saved" content.

**Content angle:** "Why I Turn Down Tear Trough Filler Requests More Than I Accept Them"

### H. Lip Flip vs. Lip Filler (SUSTAINED FAQ DRIVER)
This comparison continues to drive search and social traffic. The lip flip (Botox to the orbicularis oris) is popular as a first injectable for women who want upper lip show without volume. Honest comparison content performs well because it helps patients self-select and builds practitioner credibility.

**Content angle:** "Lip Flip or Lip Filler — I'll Tell You Which One You Actually Need"

### I. Menopause Aesthetics (EMERGING, HIGH TRUST SIGNAL)
Accelerated collagen loss, skin laxity, and volume deflation in perimenopause and menopause is being openly discussed for the first time. Practitioners who speak to this cohort directly — acknowledging the hormonal dimension, not just selling treatments — are building deep loyalty. Sculptra, Profhilo, and conservative HA filler are the clinical tools for this audience.

**Content angle:** "What Menopause Does to Your Face — And What We Can Actually Do About It"
