## Lesson 1: Quick Deep-Dive into Thumbpulling

[Image: Sobo_1909_100_-_Palatine_process_of_maxilla.png]

#### Thumbpulling is a technique that supposedly expands the sutures of the palate.

---

#### There are two main sutures "Thumbpulling" is thought to expand:

1. The median palatine suture.
2. The transverse palatine suture.

#### The neat thing is: It does fuck all at expanding those sutures.

The MSE (Maxillary Skeletal Expander), which thumbpulling is based on, is a mouth appliance used to widen these sutures with constant pressure.

[Image: 65d587d0f0e4e3b5f9e03bec_KOVAL_2087.webp]

Funny how all those individuals posting Thumbpulling transformations don't even have a tooth gap to begin with.

The difference between both is, with Thumbpulling you only apply short intense pressure to pull the sutures apart. Mechanotransduction is a physiological process that takes time, pulling once or twice daily on your sutures won't change a molecule.

Apart from that, soft tissue and other facial bones simply put your pallate back into place. That also explains why results with MSE tend to go back to baseline after some time without overexpansion.

---

#### Also no, your mandible won't grow along with your maxilla.

> 
*The mandible grew in length twice as much as the maxilla from ages 6 to 20.*

This suggests that maxillary and mandibular development aren’t fully linearly related, but rather occur independently of each other.

[https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14765052/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14765052/)

## Lesson 2: MSE and palate expanders

### ***Just looksmin theory.***

[Image: grafik.png]

#### Many individuals think that widening your palate results in aesthetically pleasing changes, this simply isn't true.

#### Just compare these results of MARPE:

- Skeletal expansion zygomatic width, **2.39 mm**.
- Nasal width, **2.68 mm**.

Expansion isn't distributed equally over the whole face, your nose width increases more than your bizygomatic width.

[https://synapse.koreamed.org/articles/1516078641](https://synapse.koreamed.org/articles/1516078641)

---

#### **Widening your palate could also result in serious asymmetries.**

[Image: MARPE-IOS-2.jpg]

Just tell me, which skull do you think mogs? Obviously the left one.

#### Like mentioned in the previous part, the results won't be fully permanent.

Imagine spending years of your life and a ton of money, just for everything to return close to baseline.

No, techniques like Thumbpulling won't change your face.
