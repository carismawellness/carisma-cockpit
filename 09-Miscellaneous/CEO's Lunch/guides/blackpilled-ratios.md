## Lesson 1: Ideal Facial Ratios - Front

## ESR - Eye Separation Ratio

ESR is measured by dividing the interpupillary distance (distance between the centres of the pupils) by the the bizygomatic width (distance from cheekbone to cheekbone).

The ideal ratio is between 0.44-0.48

[Image: image.png]

---

## Facial thirds (%)

The upper third (height between the hairline to the middle part of the eyebrows), middle third (height between the middle part of the eyebrows to the bottom of the nose) and lower third (height between the bottom of the nose to bottom of the chin) of the face.

The ideal should be approximately 33% each

[Image: image.png]

---

## Facial Width to Height Ratio (FWHR)

The FWHR is measured by dividing the bizygomatic width (distance from cheekbone to cheekbone) by the height of the middle part of the eyebrow to the upper lip.

The ideal ratio should be 1.9-2.06

[Image: image.png]

---

## Jaw frontal angle

This measurement is the inclination (in degrees) of the jaw. It is measured as shown below.

The ideal angle is between 84.5-95 degrees

[Image: image.png]

---

## Cheekbone height

This is going to be measured by dividing the distance from the upper lip to the height of your pupils by the distance from your upper lip to the widest part of your cheekbones.

Ideally this ratio should be between 80 and 100%

[Image: image.png]

---

## Total Facial Width to Height ratio

This is going to be measured by dividing the distance from the bottom of the chin to the hairline, and dividing that by the width of the widest point of your cheekbones.

The ideal ratio will fall into the range of 1.33-1.38 with 1.35 being the most ideal

[Image: image.png]

---

## Bigonial width (%)

This is going to be the width of your jaw measured at your mouth line, divided by distance of the widest point of your cheekbones multiplied by 100.

The ideal ratio is going to be 85.5%-94%

[Image: image.png]

---

## Chin to philtrum ratio

Is measured by dividing the height of the middle part of the lips to the bottom of the chin by the height of the bottom of the nose to the middle part of the lips.

The ideal ratio is between 2.05 - 2.55

[Image: image.png]

---

## Neck width (%)

Is measured by dividing the width of your neck by the width of your jaw and multiply by 100.

The ideal ratio is between 90 and 100%

[Image: image.png]

---

## Mouth width to nose width ratio

Measured by dividing the width of the mouth by the width of the nose.
The ideal ratio is between 1.38, and 1.53

[Image: image.png]

---

## Midface ratio

Measured by dividing the interpupillary distance (distance between the centres of the pupils) by the height of the nasion to the upper lip. 

The ideal ratio is between 0.97 and 1.1

[Image: image.png]

---

## Eye spacing ratio

Measured by dividing the distance between the inner point of each eye by the PFL

Ideal ratio is between 0.93 and 1.04

[Image: image.png]

---

## Eye aspect ratio

Measured by dividing the PFL by the PFH

The ideal ratio is between 2.8 and 3.5

[Image: image.png]

---

## Lower lip to upper lip ratio

Measured by dividing the height of the lower lip to that of the lower lip.

The ideal ratio is between 1.4 and 2

 

[Image: image.png]

---

## Eyebrow tilt

Measured by drawing a line following the brow inclination and a straight line.

The ideal ratio is between 5 and 13 degrees 

 

[Image: image.png]

---

## Lower 3rd proportions

Measured by dividing the distance from the lip line to the nose by the distance from the lip line to the chin and multiplied by 100.

Ideal ratio is between 31% and 40%

[Image: image.png]

---

## Canthal tilt

The inclination of the eyes in degrees.

The ideal canthal tilt is between 1 and 7 degrees.

[Image: image.png]


## Lesson 2: Ideal Facial Ratios - Side

## Gonial angle 

This is the angle formed by the junction of the posterior and lower borders of the lower jaw. Meaning it is the angle made between the mandible and the ramus.

The ideal angle is going to be between 112 and 123 degrees.

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280.png]

---

## Nasofrontal angle 

This is the place where nose meets glabella. The angle is measured as shown below.

The ideal angle is going to be between 106 and 129 degrees.

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280 (1).png]

---

## Ramus to mandible ratio

This is going to be the length of the ramus divided by the length of the mandible.

The ideal ratio is going to be between 0.59 and 0.75

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280 (7).png]

---

## Nasofacial angle

The inner angle formed by the intersection of the facial plane (glabella to pogonion) and the nasal dorsal plane.

The ideal angle is between 30 and 36 degrees.

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280 (9).png]

---

## Nasolabial angle

The angle from made by the point to the bottom of the nose to the top of the lip.

The ideal angle is going to be between 94 and 117 degrees.

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280 (2).png]

---

## Orbital vector

This is how much the area under your eye protrudes.

Having a positive orbital vector is going to be ideal. Neutral is also OK.

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280 (8).png]

[Image: image.png]

---

## Total facial convexity

This is the angle measured by having one point at the chin, one at the tip or the nose 

The ideal angle is between 137 and 149.

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280 (4).png]

---

## Mentolabial angle

This is the anterior angle formed by the intersection of a tangent to the lower lip to the upper part of the soft tissue chin pad.

The ideal angle is going to be between 108 and 130 degrees

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280 (3).png]

---

## Nasal angle

This is the angle of the nasal tip as shown below.

The ideal angle is going to be between 112 and 125 degrees.

[Image: tumblr_p58ugx3ioX1x8yz2ko1_1280 (5).png]


## Lesson 3: Physique ratios

## Shoulder to waist ratio

[Image: image.png]

This ratio is responsible for the V-taper. An ideal ratio is around 1.6-1.8. The closer to 1.8 the more ideal in my opinion. It gives a visual of an upper body with great size and proportion. This applies with and without clothes

---

## Waist to height ratio

[Image: image.png]

Ideally the waist circumference should be less than 0.45x the height. This is important because having a smaller waist will make everything "pop" more, and will make shoulders appear broader, legs appear longer and just gives the illusion of more size in general.

---

## Chest to waist ratio

[Image: image.png]

The ideal Chest to Waist ratio is going to be 1.4-1.5. This measurement matters a lot for aesthetics. Ideally speaking, a wide and thick chest over a narrow waist shows the illusion of size. It contributes to the torso looking powerful and makes the waist tighter enhancing the effect of the taper of the upper body.
