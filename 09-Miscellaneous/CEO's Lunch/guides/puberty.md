## Lesson 1: You Only Get One Puberty.

Puberty · Module 1 of 10

[Image: image.png]

This is the single most important module in the entire course. I don't care how old you are right now. If you're between 10 and 18, what you do in the next few years will permanently decide how your face looks, how tall you are, and how wide your frame gets. That's not motivation talk. That's biology.

Puberty is the only window in your life where your bones are actively reshaping themselves under hormonal instruction. Growth plates are open. Your maxilla, mandible, brow ridge, cheekbones, clavicles, everything is still being built. After this window closes, you're working with what you got. Surgery or cope.

The difference between an optimized puberty and an unoptimized one is the difference between forward growth and downward growth. Between a wide frame and a narrow one. Between ascending naturally and spending $30k on jaw surgery at 25 trying to fix what bad habits ruined at 14.

**You don't get a second shot at this.**

———

**What Actually Gets Decided During Puberty**

[Image: image.png]

Here's what's on the table right now and will never be on the table again:
- **Your final adult height.** Growth plates close. Done. No amount of stretching or HGH after the fact changes this meaningfully.
- **Your craniofacial structure.** Jaw width, brow ridge, cheekbone projection, maxilla position, gonial angle. All being shaped right now by hormones, nutrition, oral posture, and breathing patterns.
- **Your frame.** Clavicular width. Ribcage size. Shoulder structure. These are set during puberty and you can't change bones after.
- **Skin thickness and texture.** Androgen exposure during puberty partially determines this for life.
- **Muscle fiber distribution.** The foundation your physique will be built on.

Read that list again. Height. Face. Frame. Skin. Those are literally the four pillars of physical attractiveness and they are ALL being decided right now. Every single one. This window is absurdly high leverage and most guys completely waste it.

———

**The Puberty Timeline**

Puberty in guys typically kicks off between ages 10 and 13 and doesn't fully complete until somewhere between 17 and 21. That's a massive window. Here's roughly what happens when:
- **Ages 10-12:** Testes start growing. GH output starts ramping up. First growth spurts. This is where the machine turns on.
- **Ages 12-14:** Testosterone starts surging. Voice starts changing. First real facial development. Growth rate accelerates. This is the critical window where optimization matters most.
- **Ages 14-16:** Peak growth velocity for most guys. Craniofacial development is in overdrive. Mandible widens, brow ridge develops, maxilla moves forward (if your habits support it). Frame is filling out.
- **Ages 16-18:** Growth slows down. Things start consolidating. Last chance to influence some of the craniofacial development. Late bloomers still have time here.
- **Ages 18-21:** Final consolidation. Growth plates closing. Some guys still get small changes here but the major window is past.

The younger you start optimizing, the more you can influence. A 12 year old who starts mewing, sleeping right, and eating properly has years of open growth plates ahead. An 18 year old has months. Both should do it, but the 12 year old's ceiling is dramatically higher.

This is the priority order. Not random. Not equal. This is ranked by impact on your final outcome.

7. **Cut processed garbage and endocrine disruptors.** Seed oils, BPA from plastics, excessive sugar. All of these suppress the hormonal environment your development depends on.
8. **Fix your posture.** Forward head posture during growth literally pushes the jaw backward and changes the direction of craniofacial development from forward to downward. Your phone habit is reshaping your skull.
9. **Start skincare basics.** Acne scars from puberty are permanent. SPF started at 14 means ten years of UV protection by the time skin aging starts mattering.

## Lesson 2: Sleep Is Your Growth Hack.

Puberty · Module 2 of 10

[Image: image.png]

I'm gonna say something that sounds dramatic but is completely true. The single most important thing you can do for your development right now is sleep. Not lift. Not mew. Not eat protein. **Sleep.**

Growth hormone is released in massive pulses during deep sleep. During puberty, those pulses are 2-3x higher than they will ever be again in your entire life. GH drives your height, your facial bone development, your muscle growth, your fat metabolism, your collagen production. It is literally the hormone that builds your final form.

And you're killing it every night you stay up on TikTok until 2am.

———

**How GH Actually Works During Sleep**

[Image: image.png]

Here's what happens when you fall asleep. In the first 2-3 hours, your brain drops into slow-wave sleep. This is the deepest phase. This is where the biggest GH pulse of the entire day gets released. Not during exercise. Not while eating. During deep sleep.

The size of that pulse during puberty is absolutely massive compared to what adults get. Your body is running a construction project and GH is the foreman. It's telling bones to grow, telling the maxilla to develop, telling the mandible to widen, telling clavicles to lengthen. All of that gets the green light during slow-wave sleep.

Now here's the part that should scare you. **Cortisol is the direct antagonist of growth hormone.** When cortisol is high, GH gets suppressed. What raises cortisol? Sleep deprivation. Chronic stress. Blue light from screens at night. So every time you're lying in bed scrolling at midnight, you're spiking cortisol, delaying melatonin, pushing back your slow-wave sleep, and shrinking that GH pulse. You are literally making yourself shorter and less developed.

———

**How Much Sleep You Actually Need**

This is not negotiable. This is the minimum.
- **Ages 10-13:** 9-11 hours. Target 10. The growth machinery is just turning on and it needs maximum fuel.
- **Ages 14-17:** 8-10 hours. Target 9. Peak growth velocity period. This is where every hour of sleep counts the most.
- **Ages 18-21:** 8-9 hours. Target 8.5. Growth is slowing but still happening. Don't slack now.

"But I function fine on 6 hours." No you don't. You've adapted to feeling like garbage. Your GH output at 6 hours versus 9 hours is not even close. You are functioning, not developing. There's a massive difference.

And the timing matters as much as the duration. Going to bed at 10pm and waking at 7am is not the same as going to bed at 2am and waking at 11am, even though both are 9 hours. Your circadian rhythm times the GH pulse. Consistent sleep and wake times maximize it. Shifting your schedule by 2+ hours on weekends tanks the quality of your slow-wave sleep.

———

**The Exact Sleep Protocol**

Do all of this. Not some of it. All of it.

**Your room:**
- **Temperature 65-68F / 18-20C.** Your body needs to drop its core temperature to enter deep sleep. Hot room = shallow sleep = smaller GH pulse. If you don't have AC, get a fan at minimum.
- **Blackout curtains.** Any light hitting your eyes suppresses melatonin. Street lights, phone chargers, anything. Make the room pitch black.
- **No screens in the bedroom.** Your phone goes on the charger in another room. If you use it as an alarm, buy a $10 alarm clock. This is a $10 investment in your entire physical development.

**Your timing:**
- **Same bedtime every single night. Same wake time every single morning.** Including weekends. I know that sounds extreme. I don't care. Your circadian rhythm is what programs the timing of your GH pulse. Every time you shift it by 2 hours on Friday and Saturday you're disrupting the pattern for the entire week.
- **No screens 60 minutes before bed.** Blue light from your phone delays melatonin onset by up to 3 hours. Three hours. Read that again. One hour of scrolling before bed can push your melatonin back to 1am even if you put the phone down at 10pm.

**Pre-sleep:**
- **No large meals within 2 hours of sleep.** Eating a big meal spikes insulin. High insulin directly suppresses the overnight GH pulse. If you need to eat, keep it small and protein-focused, not a massive plate of carbs.
- **No intense exercise within 2 hours of sleep.** Raises core temp and cortisol. Both delay sleep onset.

[Image: image.png]

———

**Supplements That Help (Safe For Your Age)**

You don't need a stack. You need two things.
- **Magnesium glycinate 300mg before bed.** Lowers cortisol. Improves slow-wave sleep quality. Most teenagers are deficient. This is the single best sleep supplement and it's safe at any age.
- **Zinc 15-25mg with dinner.** Co-factor for both testosterone and growth hormone production. Also commonly deficient in teenagers. Take it with food or it'll make you nauseous on an empty stomach.

That's it. Don't go buying melatonin. Don't take random sleep stacks from Amazon. Your body produces melatonin naturally when you follow the protocol above. External melatonin at your age can mess with your natural production. Just fix your environment and take the mag and zinc.

———

**What's Destroying Your Sleep Right Now**
- **Your phone in bed.** This is the number one puberty killer in the modern world. Every guy in here who stays up scrolling until midnight or later is trading height and facial development for content they won't remember tomorrow. Put the phone in another room at your bedtime. Full stop.
- **Weekend sleep schedule shifts.** Staying up until 3am Friday and Saturday then sleeping until noon. You just disrupted your circadian rhythm for the entire week. Your GH output Monday through Thursday is compromised because you trashed your sleep timing over the weekend.
- **Eating a huge meal before bed.** That late night snack run, the giant bowl of cereal at 11pm. Spikes insulin. Suppresses the GH pulse. If you're hungry before bed, eat something small with protein. Not a feast.
- **Energy drinks and caffeine.** Caffeine has a half life of 5-6 hours. That Monster at 4pm still has half its caffeine active at 10pm. If you drink caffeine, cut it off by noon. Ideally don't drink it at all during puberty. You shouldn't need it if you're sleeping enough.

———

Sleep is not a nice-to-have. It is the delivery system for the most powerful growth hormone pulse you will ever experience. Every hour you shortchange it, you are leaving permanent development on the table. Set a bedtime. Kill the screens. Make the room cold and dark. Take your mag and zinc. Do this every single night for the rest of puberty and you will express more of your genetic potential than 95% of guys your age who are staying up destroying theirs.


## Lesson 3: Eat To Grow. Period.

Puberty · Module 3 of 10

[Image: image.png]

If you are currently going through puberty and you are trying to cut weight, get abs, or eat in a deficit, **stop immediately.** I'm dead serious. You are sabotaging your own development for an aesthetic goal you can achieve literally any time later in life.

Puberty is a caloric surplus phase. No exceptions. Your body is simultaneously building bone, muscle, organs, your entire craniofacial structure, and growing you taller. All at the same time. Every single one of those processes requires energy and building materials. If you don't provide them, the construction slows down or stops. And some of it never restarts.

The guys who come out of puberty with wide frames and developed faces ate enough. The guys who restricted calories at 14 for abs they lost by 16 anyway are now stuck with a frame and face that never fully developed. **You can get lean at 20. You can't reopen growth plates at 20.**

———

**How Much You Need To Eat**

These are minimums. Not targets. Minimums.
- **Ages 10-13, moderately active:** 2,200-2,600 calories per day
- **Ages 14-16, moderately active:** 2,600-3,200 calories per day
- **Ages 14-16, very active (lifting + sports):** 3,000-3,800 calories per day
- **Ages 17-20, moderately active:** 2,600-3,000 calories per day

If you're lifting 3-4 times a week and playing a sport, you probably need closer to 3,500+. Most of you are massively undereating and don't even know it. If you're not gaining weight steadily during your growth period, you're not eating enough. Simple as that.

You should be eating 200-400 calories above your TDEE at minimum. Use an online TDEE calculator, get your number, add 300. That's your daily target. Track it for a week to calibrate, then you can eyeball it after.

———

**What To Eat**

Forget complicated meal plans. Here's the priority system.

**Protein: 1.6-2.0g per kg of bodyweight. Every day. Non-negotiable.**

This is the building material for muscle, bone matrix, collagen, literally everything structural your body is constructing right now. If you weigh 60kg, that's 96-120g of protein minimum per day. If you weigh 75kg, that's 120-150g.

Best sources: steak, ground beef, chicken, eggs, fish, Greek yogurt, whole milk, cottage cheese. If you're eating 3-5 meals a day with a solid protein source at each one, you'll hit this easily.

[Image: image.png]

**The priority nutrients that actually matter during puberty:**
- **Calcium: 1,300mg per day.** Your bones are growing right now and peak bone mass gets set during adolescence. If you don't deposit enough calcium now, you have lower bone density for life. Milk, yogurt, cheese, leafy greens. A glass of milk has about 300mg. Four glasses a day and you're basically there.
- **Vitamin D: 1,000-2,000 IU daily.** You need this to absorb the calcium. It's also a co-factor for testosterone production. Get 20-30 minutes of direct sunlight daily. If that's not happening (winter, indoor lifestyle), supplement.
- **Zinc: 11mg minimum per day.** Rate-limiting for testosterone synthesis. One serving of red meat gets you there. If you don't eat red meat regularly, supplement 15mg with dinner.
- **Iron: 11mg per day.** Oxygen transport, energy production, growth. Red meat is the best source by far. If you don't eat red meat, you need to be intentional about this.
- **Magnesium: 400mg per day.** Involved in over 300 enzymatic processes including sleep quality and testosterone production. Nuts, seeds, leafy greens. Most teenagers are deficient. Supplement 300mg glycinate before bed.
- **Omega-3s.** Brain development, inflammation control. Eat fatty fish 2-3 times per week. Salmon, sardines, mackerel. If you hate fish, take a quality fish oil supplement.

———

**A Real Day Of Eating**

Here's what a solid day looks like for a 15 year old who weighs 65kg and lifts:

[Image: image.png]

**Breakfast:** 4 eggs scrambled, 2 slices of toast with butter, glass of whole milk, banana. (~700 cal, 35g protein)

**Lunch:** Rice with chicken thighs, vegetables, avocado. Glass of milk. (~800 cal, 45g protein)

**Post-workout snack:** Greek yogurt with berries, handful of almonds, protein shake if needed. (~500 cal, 35g protein)

**Dinner:** Steak or ground beef, potatoes or rice, big serving of vegetables, glass of milk. (~900 cal, 50g protein)

**Before bed (if needed):** Cottage cheese with honey. (~200 cal, 20g protein)

Total: ~3,100 calories, ~185g protein, plenty of calcium from the milk, zinc and iron from the red meat, magnesium from the nuts and greens. Done.

This isn't complicated. It's just consistent. Eat like this every day and your body has everything it needs to build.

———

**What Will Wreck Your Development**
- **Crash dieting / calorie restriction.** This is the absolute worst thing you can do during puberty. Crashes leptin, which is the master signal that tells your body it's safe to grow and develop. Low leptin = suppressed GH, suppressed testosterone, delayed development. Permanent consequences. Do not cut during puberty.
- **Living on junk food.** High calories from trash food gives you the energy but not the building materials. Your body needs protein, calcium, zinc, iron. A diet of pizza, chips, and soda gives you calories with none of the micronutrients that actually build bone and muscle. You'll gain fat, not development.
- **Skipping breakfast.** After an overnight fast, your body is in a catabolic state. Morning nutrition signals that resources are available and development can proceed. Intermittent fasting is for adults optimizing body composition. Not for teenagers trying to grow.
- **Excessive sugar and seed oils.** Sugar spikes insulin, causes glycation, promotes inflammation. Seed oils are pro-inflammatory. Both disrupt the hormonal environment your development depends on. Cook with butter, olive oil, or coconut oil. Drink water instead of soda.
- **Alcohol.** I'll say it in every module because it's that important. Alcohol is directly toxic to the cells that produce testosterone during development. Zero alcohol during puberty. Not "a little." Zero.

———

You can optimize your body composition later. You can cut, bulk, do whatever you want with your diet once your growth plates close and your face is done developing. But right now, during puberty, the only dietary goal is **feed the machine.** High protein, high calcium, adequate calories, whole foods. Every day. No days off. Your future face and frame depend on what you eat this year.


## Lesson 4: Mewing Done Right.

Puberty · Module 4 of 10

[Image: image.png]

Mewing is the most talked about and most poorly understood topic in this entire space. Half the internet thinks it's a magic bullet that gives you a jawline in 6 months. The other half thinks it's complete pseudoscience. Both are wrong.

Here's the truth. Correct oral posture is backed by mainstream orthodontic science. The mechanism is real. Your tongue exerts force on your palate. During development when your midface sutures are still open, that force influences how your maxilla, palate, and cheekbones grow. Forward and wide, or downward and narrow. That's real.

What's NOT real is the idea that a 25 year old can reshape their skull by pressing their tongue to the roof of their mouth for 6 months. Adult bones are fused. The window for meaningful structural change through oral posture alone is during growth. Which is why this module is in the puberty course.

**If you are between 10 and 18, mewing is one of the highest leverage things you can do for your face. If you are over 22, the structural impact is minimal.** That's the honest answer nobody wants to give you because it doesn't get clicks.

———

**What Mewing Actually Does During Puberty**

When your tongue rests flat on the palate with gentle consistent upward pressure, it acts as an orthopedic force on the maxilla. During puberty, the midpalatal suture is still open and the midface sutures are still actively fusing. This means:
- **The palate can widen.** Tongue pressure spreads the two halves of the palate apart. Wider palate = wider dental arch = wider smile = wider base for cheekbones.
- **The maxilla can grow forward.** Upward and forward tongue pressure encourages the maxilla to develop in a forward direction rather than dropping downward. Forward maxilla = better cheekbone projection, better under-eye support, shorter midface.
- **The mandible follows.** When the maxilla grows forward, the mandible tends to follow because the bite has to match. Better maxilla position = better jaw position.

This is the same mechanism that makes mouth breathing so destructive. When the tongue drops and the mouth hangs open, you remove all that upward pressure from the palate. The palate narrows. The maxilla grows downward. The mandible rotates backward. Long face, recessed jaw, narrow palate. That's what happens when you do the opposite of mewing for years.

[Image: image.png]

———

**Exact Tongue Placement (Step By Step)**

Most of you are going to get this wrong the first time. That's fine. Here's exactly how to find the right position.

**Step 1: Find the spot.**

Say the word "sing" out loud. Hold the "ng" sound. Feel where your tongue is? The back of your tongue is pressed up against the soft palate behind your hard palate. That's approximately where the back third of your tongue needs to live. Now say the letter "N" and hold it. Feel the tip of your tongue pressing behind your front teeth against the ridge? That's where the tip goes. The entire tongue from tip to back third should be flat against the roof of your mouth.

**Step 2: Place the tongue.**

Start with the tip. Place it against the ridge just behind your upper front teeth. Not touching the teeth themselves, about half a centimeter behind them. Now roll the rest of your tongue upward until the entire surface is pressed flat against the palate. **The back third is the hardest part and the most important part.** Most people only get the tip up there. That does almost nothing. The force that influences maxilla development comes from the back third pressing upward.

**Step 3: Check your placement.**

With your tongue pressed up, try to swallow. If you can swallow with your tongue staying on the palate the entire time, your placement is correct. If your tongue drops during the swallow, the back third isn't engaged. Try again.

Another check: look in a mirror and open your mouth. Your tongue should be fully suctioned to the palate. You should be able to see the bottom of your tongue and the underside should be visible because the whole thing is pulled up. If you can see the top surface of your tongue, it's not up there.

**Step 4: Close your lips.**

Lips together, naturally. No strain, no effort. Just closed. If your lips won't stay closed comfortably, you probably have a tongue posture or breathing issue that needs addressing first (see Module 5 on nasal breathing).

**Step 5: Teeth position.**

Lightly touching or barely apart. NOT clenched. If you catch yourself clenching your jaw, you're doing it wrong. Clenching causes TMJ problems and has nothing to do with mewing. Light contact or slight gap. Relaxed.

**Step 6: Breathe through your nose.**

If you can't breathe through your nose with your tongue on the palate, you have a nasal obstruction issue. Fix that first (Module 5). Mewing while mouth breathing is completely pointless because the tongue drops every time you open your mouth to breathe.

[Image: image.png]

———

**What It Should Feel Like**

When you first do this correctly, it will feel weird. Here's how you know you're doing it right:
- **You feel a slight suction.** The tongue creates a mild vacuum seal against the palate. This is correct. It should feel like the tongue is gently stuck up there, not that you're forcefully pressing it.
- **Your face feels slightly "lifted."** Some people describe it as a gentle upward pull in the cheekbone area. This is the tongue pressure being transmitted through the palate to the midface. Good sign.
- **Swallowing is slightly different.** You'll swallow with the tongue staying on the palate, using a wave motion rather than the tongue pushing forward. This is correct swallowing. Most people have an incorrect swallow pattern where the tongue pushes against the front teeth.
- **Your jaw feels more relaxed, not more tense.** If your jaw feels tense or sore, you're clenching. Back off. Mewing is about tongue position, not jaw clenching.
- **It's tiring at first.** The muscles at the back of the tongue are weak if you've never used them this way. It's normal for the tongue to fatigue and drop after 10-20 minutes initially. It builds up over weeks.

———

**How To Make It Unconscious**

The goal is NOT to consciously mew 24/7. The goal is to retrain your resting tongue posture so your tongue naturally sits on the palate without thinking about it. Here's the progression:
- **Week 1-2:** Consciously place your tongue on the palate as often as you remember. Set hourly reminders on your phone if you need to. Every time you catch your tongue resting low, put it back up.
- **Week 3-4:** You'll start catching yourself in correct position without having placed it there consciously. The muscle memory is building. Keep correcting whenever you notice it's dropped.
- **Month 2-3:** For most people, this is where it becomes the default resting position. You'll catch yourself mewing without thinking about it. When this happens, you've successfully retrained the habit.
- **Month 3+:** Maintenance. It should be automatic. If you notice it's dropped, just place it back. But by now it's rare.

The whole process takes about 2-3 months of consistent effort. After that it's just your natural posture.

———

**When To Expect Results**

This depends entirely on your age. Let me be honest with you because most content about this is not.
- **Ages 10-15:** This is the golden window. Sutures are fully open. Consistent mewing over 6-12 months during active growth can produce visible changes in palate width, cheekbone prominence, and jaw position. The younger you start, the more dramatic the potential.
- **Ages 15-18:** Still effective but the window is narrowing. Sutures are starting to fuse. 12-24 months of consistent mewing during this period can still produce noticeable changes, especially combined with proper nutrition and sleep that fuel the growth.
- **Ages 18-22:** Mostly soft tissue changes. Slightly better jaw definition from the muscles, potentially minor postural shifts. Don't expect bone remodeling. Still worth doing for the muscle and posture benefits.
- **Ages 22+:** Minimal structural impact. Good for muscle tone and posture. Not going to reshape your maxilla. If you want structural change at this age, you're looking at orthodontic devices or surgery.

**If you're under 16 and reading this, you have a massive advantage.** Start now. Do it right. Be consistent. The next 2-4 years of correct oral posture during your peak growth window is worth more than any amount of mewing at 25.

———

**What NOT To Do**
- **Hard mewing.** Forcefully jamming your tongue against the palate. This causes TMJ issues, headaches, and asymmetric pressure that can actually make things worse. The force should be gentle and consistent, like your tongue is resting there, not like you're trying to crack your skull open from the inside.
- **Only putting the tip up.** The tip is the least important part. If the back third of your tongue isn't on the palate, you're getting almost zero benefit. The structural force comes from the back third.
- **Clenching your jaw.** Mewing and jaw clenching are completely different things. Teeth lightly touching or slightly apart. If your masseters are sore, you're clenching, not mewing.
- **Mewing with your mouth open.** If you're mouth breathing, your tongue drops every time you inhale. Fix nasal breathing first. This is covered in the next module.
- **Expecting overnight results.** Bone remodeling is measured in months and years. If someone shows you a "2 week mewing transformation" it's lighting, angles, or just a lie.

———

Mewing is not magic and it's not fake. It's correct oral posture that your tongue should have been doing your whole life. During puberty, getting this right means your maxilla, palate, and midface develop forward and wide instead of down and narrow. Start now, make it unconscious within 2-3 months, and let your growth do the rest. Combined with nasal breathing, proper sleep, and good nutrition, this is how you express the best version of your craniofacial genetics.


## Lesson 5: Close Your Mouth.

Puberty · Module 5 of 10

[Image: image.png]

Mouth breathing during development is probably the single most destructive habit for your face. This isn't opinion. They literally tested this on primates. Blocked their noses, forced them to mouth breathe, and watched their faces grow longer, narrower, and more recessed over time. The results were brutal.

And it's the exact same mechanism in humans. Every year you spend mouth breathing during puberty is a year of your face growing in the wrong direction. Narrow palate. Long face. Recessed jaw. Flat cheekbones. The classic mouth breather face is real and it's the result of years of your tongue sitting on the floor of your mouth instead of the roof.

**If you can't breathe through your nose comfortably right now, this is priority number one.**Before mewing. Before anything else in this course. Fix the airway first.

———

**What Mouth Breathing Does To Your Face**

Here's the chain of events that happens when you breathe through your mouth during development:

1. **Your tongue drops from the palate.** You can't keep your tongue on the roof of your mouth and breathe through your mouth at the same time. Try it. Impossible. So the tongue drops to the floor of the mouth.
2. **The palate loses its widening force.** Without the tongue pressing up, the palate has nothing pushing it wider. It narrows over time.
3. **Your lips part and your jaw drops.** Open mouth = jaw drops open = jaw rotates downward and backward.
4. **The midface grows downward instead of forward.** Without the upward force from the tongue and with the jaw pulling downward, the whole midface elongates vertically. Long face syndrome.
5. **The mandible rotates backward.** As the midface drops, the lower jaw follows and rotates into a more recessed position. Weak chin. Poor gonial angle. Recessed jawline.

Do this for 5-10 years during development and you end up with a permanently different face than you would have had breathing through your nose. Narrower. Longer. More recessed. Less defined cheekbones. Worse jaw. It's a failo factory.

[Image: image.png]

———

**Why You Might Be Mouth Breathing**

Before you can fix it, you need to figure out what's causing it. Here are the common causes ranked by how often I see them:
- **Habit.** The most common. You've just always done it. Nobody corrected you. Your tongue sits low, your mouth stays open, especially at night. There's no structural problem, just a bad habit that got reinforced for years. This is the easiest to fix.
- **Chronic allergies.** Allergic rhinitis makes your nose stuffy so you default to mouth breathing. Extremely common. If your nose is always congested, especially seasonally, this is probably you.
- **Enlarged adenoids or tonsils.** These physically block the airway. Very common in kids and teenagers. If you've always struggled to breathe through your nose, even when you're not sick, get this checked.
- **Deviated septum.** The wall between your nostrils is crooked, restricting airflow on one or both sides. You might notice one nostril is always more blocked than the other.
- **Nasal polyps.** Growths inside the nasal passages that block airflow. Less common in teenagers but possible.

**If nasal breathing is consistently difficult, see an ENT (ear nose and throat doctor) before trying to force behavioral fixes.** If there's a structural problem, no amount of willpower fixes it. Get it assessed. Adenoid removal, septoplasty, or allergy treatment might be what stands between you and proper facial development.

———

**The Fix Protocol**

**Step 1: Rule out structural issues.**

Can you breathe through your nose comfortably when you consciously try? If yes, you probably just have a habit issue. If no, go see an ENT. Tell them you have difficulty breathing through your nose and you want to address it. They'll scope your nasal passages and figure out what's going on.

**Step 2: Handle allergies.**

If your nose is chronically stuffy, try an OTC nasal corticosteroid spray like fluticasone (Flonase). Use it daily for 2-4 weeks. This is the first-line treatment for allergic rhinitis and it works for most people. It takes about a week to fully kick in, so be patient. If allergies are severe, see a doctor about antihistamines or allergy testing.

**Step 3: Conscious nasal breathing during the day.**

Set reminders on your phone every hour. When the reminder goes off, check: is my mouth closed? Am I breathing through my nose? Is my tongue on the palate? If not, correct it. Do this for 2-3 weeks and it starts becoming automatic.

**Step 4: Mouth taping at night.**

This is the big one. Most habitual mouth breathers breathe through their nose during the day but default to mouth breathing while they sleep. You're unconscious for 8-10 hours and your mouth is hanging open the whole time. That's a third of your life with your tongue off the palate and your jaw dropping.

Here's how to do it safely:
- Use gentle medical paper tape. Not duct tape. Not athletic tape. Medical paper tape like 3M Micropore.
- Take a small strip (about 2 inches) and place it vertically over the center of your lips.
- You can still open your mouth slightly if you need to. The tape is gentle enough to break free in an emergency. It's a reminder, not a lock.
- **Only do this if you can breathe through your nose comfortably when lying down.** If you have a structural obstruction, fix that first. Don't tape your mouth shut if you can't breathe through your nose.
- Start with just naps or the first few hours of sleep to get comfortable with it.
 
[Image: image.png]

**Step 5: Nasal breathing during exercise.**

Train yourself to breathe through your nose during low-intensity exercise. Walking, light jogging, easy lifting sets. It feels uncomfortable at first because your body is used to the larger airflow from mouth breathing. Within 2-3 weeks of consistent practice, your nasal passages adapt and widen. You'll be able to handle more intensity with nasal breathing over time.

For high-intensity stuff like heavy compound lifts or sprints, mouth breathing is fine. You're not going to mess up your face during a set of heavy squats. This is about your resting breathing pattern, not your 1RM.

———

**The Nasal Breathing Benefits Beyond Face**

Your nose isn't just a tube for air. It does things your mouth can't:
- **Filters particles and pathogens.** Nose hairs and mucous membranes trap dust, bacteria, and allergens. Your mouth does none of this.
- **Warms and humidifies the air.** Cold, dry air hitting your lungs directly is inflammatory. The nose conditions the air first.
- **Produces nitric oxide.** Your sinuses produce NO when you breathe through your nose. NO is a vasodilator that improves oxygen uptake in the lungs by 10-15%. More oxygen per breath. Better performance. Better recovery.

———

**The Timeline**
- **Week 1-2:** Conscious effort. Feels weird. You keep catching yourself mouth breathing and correcting. Normal.
- **Week 3-4:** Daytime nasal breathing starts feeling natural. Mouth taping at night is comfortable. You wake up with your mouth closed most mornings.
- **Month 2-3:** Nasal breathing is your default. You catch mouth breathing occasionally but it feels wrong now. The habit has flipped.
- **Month 3+:** Fully automatic. Your tongue rests on the palate, lips stay closed, nose does the breathing. This is now your permanent state. Combined with mewing, your oral posture is locked in.

———

Nasal breathing and mewing are two halves of the same thing. You can't mew while mouth breathing and you can't mouth breathe while mewing. Fix the breathing first. Once your airway is clear and nasal breathing is your default, correct oral posture follows naturally. If you've been mouth breathing for years, the fix starts today. See an ENT if you need to. Tape your mouth at night. Set your hourly reminders. Every day you spend nasal breathing during puberty is a day your face is growing in the right direction.


## Lesson 6: Lifting Won't Stunt You.

Puberty · Module 6 of 10

[Image: image.png]

"Won't lifting weights stunt my growth?" No. It won't. That myth needs to die. It's been studied extensively and there is zero evidence that properly performed resistance training damages growth plates or stunts height. Zero. What stunts your growth is sitting on the couch through the most anabolic period of your life.

Resistance training during puberty does the exact opposite of stunting you. It stimulates growth hormone and testosterone acutely after every session. It increases bone density because bones adapt to load by getting stronger. It builds the shoulder, back, and neck musculature that literally defines your adult frame. And it establishes habits that will compound for the rest of your life.

The guys who start lifting at 14-15 with good form and consistency come out of puberty with wider shoulders, thicker necks, better posture, and higher bone density than the guys who waited until college. **This is one of the biggest head starts you can give yourself.**

———

**What To Prioritize (This Matters)**

Not all muscle groups are equal when you're young and trying to build an aesthetic frame. Here's what to focus on, ranked by impact on how you look at 20:

[Image: image.png]

1. **Shoulders (lateral deltoids).** This is the single highest ROI muscle group for frame. Wide shoulders mog everything. Your clavicle length is genetic and fixed, but the deltoid muscle that sits on top of those clavicles can add significant visual width. Lateral raises should be in every single workout.
2. **Upper back and lats.** The V-taper foundation. Wide lats make your waist look narrow and your shoulders look even wider. Rows, pulldowns, pull-ups. This is what separates a guy with a frame from a guy who just has arms.
3. **Neck.** Massively underrated. A thick neck completely changes how your face and jaw look. It frames the lower third, makes your jaw angle more visible, and makes you look more masculine from every angle. Neck curls, neck extensions, shrugs. Start light. Be consistent.
4. **Chest.** Fills out the upper body, supports good posture. Important but not as high priority as shoulders and back for frame building.
5. **Legs and glutes.** Foundation, proportionality, and the hormonal stimulus from heavy compound leg work is massive. Don't skip legs. Ever.

Notice what's NOT at the top? Arms. Biceps and triceps will grow from the compound movements. You don't need a dedicated arm day at 15. You need shoulders, back, and neck. That's what builds the frame that makes everything else look good.

———

**The Starter Program**

If you've never lifted before or you've been messing around without a real program, here's what your first 6-12 months should look like.

**3 days per week. Full body each day. Technique before load.**

Here are the exercises. Learn these. Do them properly. Don't ego lift.

1. **Goblet squat** (or bodyweight squat until you learn the pattern) — 3 sets x 10 reps
2. **Romanian deadlift with dumbbells** (light, learn the hip hinge) — 3 sets x 10 reps
3. **Dumbbell bench press** — 3 sets x 10 reps
4. **Dumbbell row** — 3 sets x 10 reps each arm
5. **Overhead press** (dumbbells or barbell) — 3 sets x 10 reps
6. **Pull-up or lat pulldown** — 3 sets x as many as you can (or 10 reps on pulldown)
7. **Lateral raise** — 3 sets x 15 reps
8. **Face pull** — 3 sets x 15 reps
9. **Neck flexion + extension** — 2 sets x 15 reps each direction

Rest 60-90 seconds between sets. Full workout should take about 45-60 minutes. Do this Monday, Wednesday, Friday or similar with a rest day between sessions.

[Image: image.png]

**Progression:** When you can do all sets and reps with good form, add a small amount of weight next session. 2.5-5 lbs on upper body lifts, 5-10 lbs on lower body. Slow and steady. The goal for the first year is to learn the movements perfectly and build the habit. The heavy weights come later.

———

**Safety Rules (Read These)**

The reason the "lifting stunts growth" myth exists is because a small number of kids injured themselves lifting heavy with terrible form. Growth plate fractures CAN happen from acute injury. They don't happen from proper training. Here's how you stay safe:
- **Learn technique before adding load.** Film yourself. Watch videos from credible sources. Better yet, get an adult who lifts to watch your form for the first few sessions. Proper form is non-negotiable.
- **No 1-rep maxing.** You have zero reason to test your max squat or bench at 14. Keep reps at 8-15 for the first year. Build the foundation. There's plenty of time for heavy lifting later.
- **Stop if you feel joint pain.** Muscle soreness the day after training is normal and means you're adapting. Sharp pain in a joint during an exercise means something is wrong. Stop. Adjust. Pain is information, not a badge of honor.
- **Don't train through illness.** Your immune system is already working overtime during puberty. If you're sick, rest. Missing a week of training costs you nothing. Training while sick delays recovery and can make things worse.
- **Warm up.** 5 minutes of light cardio and some mobility work before you touch weights. Your joints and tendons are still developing. Respect that.

———

**What Most Young Guys Get Wrong**
- **Ego lifting.** Loading up the bar to impress people with a half-rep bench press. Nobody cares. You're building muscle to build a frame, not to post a number on Instagram. Full range of motion, controlled tempo, proper form. Every rep.
- **Arms and chest only.** The classic bro split at 15. Chest and biceps Monday, chest and biceps Thursday, never touch a lateral raise or a row. This is how you end up with a narrow frame, rounded shoulders, and no V-taper. Shoulders and back first. Always.
- **Skipping neck.** Your neck is one of the most visible muscles from every angle. A thick neck makes your jaw look more defined, makes your frame look bigger, and adds a massive amount of perceived masculinity. 5 minutes at the end of each workout. Neck curls and extensions with a plate or a harness. No excuses.
- **Training once a week.** One session per week is basically maintenance. You need minimum 3 sessions for real adaptation during puberty. Your recovery capacity right now is higher than it'll ever be. Use it.
- **Not eating enough.** If you're training 3x a week and not eating in a surplus with high protein, you're spinning your wheels. Training is the stimulus. Food is the fuel. Without the fuel, the stimulus does almost nothing. Read Module 3 again if you haven't.

———

Start lifting now. Three days a week. Learn the movements. Focus on shoulders, back, and neck. Eat enough to support the training. Don't ego lift. Don't skip sessions. In 12 months you'll have a frame and posture that mogs 90% of guys your age who are still sitting around doing nothing. And you'll have built that foundation during the most anabolic, most responsive period of your entire life. That's an advantage you can never get back once it's gone.


## Lesson 7: Stop Killing Your Hormones.

Right now, during puberty, your testosterone, growth hormone, DHT, and IGF-1 are running at the highest levels they will ever naturally reach in your entire life. This is not a phase you need to boost. This is a phase you need to stop sabotaging.

Go through this list honestly. If you're doing any of these, you are actively suppressing the hormones that are building your face, height, and frame right now.

[Image: image.png]

I know "manage your stress" sounds like something a school counselor would say. But cortisol is a real hormone that directly suppresses testosterone and growth hormone. Chronic stress during puberty has measurable developmental consequences. Here's what actually works:

## Lesson 8: Your Skin Is A Halo Or A Failo.

Puberty · Module 8 of 10

[Image: image.png]

Puberty means acne for most of you. Androgens are surging, your skin is producing more oil than it ever has, and your pores are getting clogged. That's just biology. You can't avoid the hormonal component. But you can absolutely control how bad it gets and whether it leaves permanent scars.

Acne scars are permanent. Let me say that again. **Acne scars are permanent.** The pitted, ice-pick, rolling scars from severe acne that goes untreated or gets picked at? Those stay with you for life. They're one of the biggest failos a face can have because they destroy skin texture and no amount of bone structure compensates for cratered skin.

The good news: acne is treatable. The even better news: starting skincare habits now means you come out of puberty with better skin than 95% of guys your age AND you've built habits that prevent premature aging for decades.

———

**Why Puberty Skin Freaks Out**

Testosterone and DHT are rising fast. DHT specifically stimulates your sebaceous glands (oil glands) to produce more sebum. More oil = more clogged pores = more food for the bacteria (C. acnes) that causes inflammation. That's the whole mechanism:

**Rising androgens → more sebum → clogged pores → bacteria → inflammation → acne.**

You can't stop the androgens and you wouldn't want to because they're building your face and body. But you can manage the downstream effects: control the oil, keep pores clear, reduce bacteria, calm inflammation.

———

**The Exact Routine (Do This Every Day)**

This is not complicated. Don't overthink it. Don't buy 15 products. Here's what you need.

**Morning:**

1. **Gentle cleanser.** CeraVe foaming or Vanicream gentle. Wet your face, use a dime-sized amount, massage for 30 seconds, rinse. Don't scrub hard. Don't use those "deep clean" charcoal washes that strip your face raw. Gentle.
2. **Lightweight moisturizer.** CeraVe PM or Vanicream daily. Yes, even if your skin is oily. Stripping oil without moisturizing makes your skin produce even MORE oil to compensate. Moisturizer keeps the barrier healthy.
3. **SPF 30-50.** Every single morning. Non-negotiable. UV damage starts accumulating the day you're born. Starting SPF at 14 means by the time you're 25, when photoaging starts becoming visible, you've got 10+ years of protection banked. Your skin will look noticeably younger than every guy your age who didn't do this. Plus SPF prevents the dark marks that acne leaves behind from getting worse.

**Evening:**

1. **Same gentle cleanser.** Wash off the day. Same method.
2. **Active treatment.** This is where you fight the acne. One of these, not all at once: undefined. **Adapalene 0.1% gel (Differin).** This is an OTC retinoid. It's the gold standard first-line treatment for mild to moderate acne. Apply a pea-sized amount to your entire face (not just breakouts) every evening. It takes 8-12 weeks to see real results. You will probably purge (get worse before better) in weeks 2-4. That's normal. Push through.
- **OR Salicylic acid 2% treatment.** If retinoids irritate you too much initially, start here. It's a BHA that penetrates pores and clears them from the inside. Less irritating than adapalene but also less effective long-term.
3. **Moisturizer.** Same as morning. Apply after the treatment has absorbed (5-10 minutes).

[Image: image.png]

———

**When To Add Benzoyl Peroxide**

If you're getting red, inflamed pimples (not just blackheads and whiteheads), add benzoyl peroxide 2.5-5% as a spot treatment or short-contact wash. BP kills the bacteria that causes inflammatory acne. You can use it in the morning (as a wash, leave on 2-3 minutes then rinse) while using adapalene at night. Don't apply BP and adapalene at the same time in the same step because BP can deactivate adapalene.

2.5% BP is just as effective as 10% with way less irritation. Don't go nuclear with high percentages. Your face isn't a science experiment.

———

**When To See A Dermatologist**

Go see a derm if any of these apply to you:
- **Cystic or nodular acne.** Deep, painful, under-the-skin bumps that don't come to a head. These leave the worst scars and OTC products can't reach them. A derm can prescribe oral treatments.
- **Your acne is leaving scars.** Don't wait. Scars are permanent. If your current approach isn't working and you're getting scarring, escalate immediately.
- **8-12 weeks of adapalene + BP hasn't helped.** You gave it a proper trial and it's still bad. Time for prescription-strength options.
- **It's affecting your confidence or mental health.** Legitimate reason. A derm can help.

The nuclear option is **isotretinoin (Accutane).** It's the most effective acne treatment that exists. It shrinks your oil glands and can permanently resolve severe acne. If your dermatologist recommends it and your acne is bad, take it seriously. The side effects (dry skin, dry lips, joint aches, monthly blood tests) are temporary. The scars from untreated severe acne are not.

**Talk to your parents about seeing a dermatologist if your acne is moderate-severe.** This is one of those things where getting professional help early prevents permanent damage. Don't be too proud to ask.

———

**Diet And Acne**

This is real and most people ignore it:
- **High glycemic foods (sugar, white bread, candy, soda) spike insulin.** Insulin increases androgen activity and sebum production. Guys who clean up their sugar intake consistently see improvement in acne. Not a cure, but a significant factor.
- **Dairy may trigger acne in some people.** Particularly skim milk. If your acne is persistent, try cutting dairy for 4-6 weeks and see what happens. If no change, add it back. If it clears up, you have your answer.
- **Omega-3 fatty acids reduce inflammation.** Eat fatty fish 2-3x per week or supplement fish oil. Anti-inflammatory eating helps skin from the inside.
- **Zinc 25-30mg supports skin healing and has mild anti-inflammatory effects.** You should already be supplementing this from Module 7. Double benefit.

———

**The Rules**
- **Do not pick your skin.** Ever. Picking spreads bacteria deeper, creates more inflammation, and causes scarring that lasts forever. When you see a pimple, leave it alone. Apply your treatment and let it heal. I know it's hard. Do it anyway. The scar from picking is 100x worse than the pimple.
- **Do not use harsh scrubs or alcohol toners.** St. Ives apricot scrub, those charcoal "deep clean" face washes, alcohol-based toners. All of these strip the skin barrier, cause micro-tears and irritation, and trigger your skin to produce even more oil. They make everything worse.
- **Be patient with treatments.** Adapalene takes 8-12 weeks. Most guys quit after 2 weeks because "it's not working" or because they're purging. The purge is the treatment working, bringing existing congestion to the surface. Push through.
- **Wear SPF every day.** Not optional. Acne marks (the dark spots left after pimples heal) get darker with sun exposure. SPF prevents this AND prevents the long-term aging that you'll care about a lot at 30.

———

Clear skin is a halo at any PSL level. Bad skin with scars is a failo that no bone structure overcomes. Start the routine now. Cleanser, moisturizer, SPF in the morning. Cleanser, adapalene, moisturizer at night. Don't pick. See a derm if it's bad. In 3-6 months you'll have noticeably better skin than most guys your age. And every year you wear SPF during your teens is a year of protection that shows up when everyone else starts looking old.


## Lesson 9: Your Phone Is Reshaping Your Skull.

Puberty · Module 9 of 10

[Image: image.png]

This one is going to make some of you uncomfortable because you're probably doing the thing I'm about to roast you for right now. Look at your posture as you read this. Is your head forward? Is your chin poking out? Are you hunched over a phone or laptop?

Forward head posture during puberty is not just a cosmetic issue. It actively influences how your skull and jaw develop. The mechanical forces acting on your face during growth are partly determined by your head position. When your head sits forward, your jaw gets pushed backward, your hyoid bone drops lower, and the direction of craniofacial growth shifts from forward to downward.

**Your phone habit is literally reshaping your skull.** During the one window where skull reshaping is actually happening.

———

**Why Posture Affects Your Face**

The skull and jaw grow in response to mechanical forces. Muscles pull on bones. Gravity pulls on bones. The position of your head changes how all of these forces interact.

**Forward head posture does this:**
- **Pushes the mandible backward.** When your head is forward, the muscles and ligaments connecting your jaw to your skull pull the mandible into a more recessed position. Over years of growth, this becomes the default position the jaw develops in. Recessed jaw. Weak chin. Worse gonial angle.
- **Drops the hyoid bone.** The hyoid is a small U-shaped bone in your upper neck. It anchors your tongue and the floor of your mouth. When it drops lower (from forward head posture), the entire jaw-neck angle gets blurred. That sharp defined angle between your jaw and neck? It requires a high hyoid. Forward head posture lowers it.
- **Shifts growth direction downward.** When the head is forward and the jaw is pulled back, the craniofacial growth pattern tilts toward vertical (downward) rather than horizontal (forward). This contributes to the long face pattern we talked about in the mewing and breathing modules.

[Image: image.png]

This isn't just theory. The research shows that cervical (neck) posture is directly associated with mandibular position and craniofacial morphology. Your body is a connected system. How you hold your head affects how your face grows.

———

**The Three Posture Problems Destroying Your Face**

**1. Forward head posture (tech neck).**

Your head weighs 10-12 pounds. When it's balanced over your spine, the load is distributed efficiently. For every inch your head sits forward, the effective load on your neck doubles. Most of you are walking around with your head 2-3 inches forward from hours of phone use. That's 30-40 pounds of effective load pulling your neck forward and your jaw backward. Every. Single. Day.

**2. Rounded shoulders.**

Chest muscles tight, upper back muscles weak. Shoulders roll forward. This makes your frame look narrower than it actually is and it feeds into the forward head posture because the two go together. You can't fix the head without fixing the shoulders.

**3. Anterior pelvic tilt.**

Less directly face-related but it affects your entire silhouette. Your pelvis tilts forward, your belly sticks out, your lower back overarches. Makes you look shorter and less athletic even if you have a decent physique. Common in guys who sit a lot.

**The Fix Protocol**

**Exercise 1: Chin tucks.**

This is the single most impactful exercise for forward head posture. Here's exactly how to do it:
- Stand with your back flat against a wall.
- Pull your chin straight back (not down) like you're trying to make a double chin. Your head should slide backward on the wall.
- Hold for 5 seconds. Release. That's one rep.
- Do 10 reps, 3-5 times per day.
- This takes 2 minutes per session. You can do it in the bathroom, between classes, at your desk. No equipment needed.

When done correctly, you should feel a stretch at the base of your skull and the back of your neck. If you feel strain in the front of your neck, you're tucking your chin down instead of pulling it back. It's a retraction movement, not a flexion movement.

**Exercise 2: Dead hangs.**
- Grab a pull-up bar with an overhand grip.
- Hang with straight arms for 30-60 seconds.
- Let your spine decompress. Let your shoulders stretch.
- 2-3 sets, daily or after every training session.

This decompresses the cervical spine, stretches the chest and shoulders, and counteracts all the compression from sitting hunched over.

**Exercise 3: Face pulls.**
- Cable machine or resistance band at face height.
- Pull toward your face with elbows high, squeezing the rear delts and upper back at the end.
- 3 sets of 15-20 reps.
- Do these in every workout. They build the rear delt and upper trap strength that holds your shoulders back and your posture upright.

**Exercise 4: Thoracic extension.**
- Lie on a foam roller placed horizontally across your upper back.
- Hands behind your head.
- Arch your upper back over the roller, extending the thoracic spine.
- Hold 2-3 seconds at the bottom. Return. Repeat 10 times.
- Do this daily. 2 minutes. Counteracts the thoracic flexion (rounding) from desk and phone use.

———

**Lifestyle Changes (These Matter More Than The Exercises)**

The exercises fix the damage. These changes prevent new damage from accumulating:
- **Phone at eye level.** Stop looking down at your phone. Hold it up. Every time. I know it looks weird at first. Your neck and jaw development don't care how it looks. This one change eliminates the most common source of forward head posture in your generation.
- **Monitor at eye level at your desk.** Top of the screen should be at or slightly below eye level. If you're on a laptop, get a separate keyboard and elevate the laptop to eye level. Or get a cheap monitor stand. This is a $15 fix for hours of daily posture destruction.
- **Hourly posture check.** Set a reminder on your phone. Every hour: are my ears directly over my shoulders? Are my shoulders back? Is my chin tucked slightly? If not, reset. This awareness is what converts the exercises into actual lasting change.
- **Sleep on your back.** Side sleeping and especially stomach sleeping can push the jaw into weird positions for 8 hours straight. Back sleeping with a thin pillow that keeps your neck in neutral is ideal. Hard to change if you're a side sleeper, but worth working on gradually.

———

**The Timeline**
- **Week 1-2:** Chin tucks feel weird. You become aware of how bad your posture actually is. Good. Awareness is the first step.
- **Week 3-4:** The posture correction exercises become routine. You start catching yourself in forward head posture and correcting without the hourly reminder.
- **Month 2-3:** Visible difference in how you carry your head. Your jaw-neck angle looks sharper. Friends might comment that you look taller or different. You're not taller, you're just not hunched anymore.
- **Month 3-6:** Good posture becomes your default. Forward head posture feels uncomfortable now. The exercises are maintenance rather than correction.

———

Every hour you spend looking down at your phone during puberty is an hour of mechanical force pushing your jaw backward and your face downward. The fix is simple: chin tucks daily, dead hangs, face pulls in every workout, phone at eye level. Takes 5-10 minutes of exercise and one lifestyle change. The alternative is arriving at 20 with a recessed jaw and soft neck angle because you spent your entire puberty looking down at a screen. Your choice.


## Lesson 10: Get Your Palate Expanded Now.

Puberty · Module 10 of 10

[Image: image.png]

This is the most underrated module in the whole course. Palate width is one of the biggest structural factors in facial aesthetics and almost nobody talks about it properly. A wide palate gives you wider cheekbones, a broader smile, better nasal airway, and enough room for your tongue to actually sit on the roof of your mouth (which makes mewing possible). A narrow palate does the opposite of all of that.

And here's the critical part: **expanding the palate during puberty is relatively simple and extremely effective.** After the midpalatal suture fuses (mid-20s), you need surgery to do what a simple orthodontic device does at 13. The window is open right now. And most of you don't even know it exists.

———

**Why Palate Width Matters For Your Face**

Your palate is the roof of your mouth. It's formed by two bones joined at the midpalatal suture down the middle. The width of your palate directly affects:
- **Cheekbone width.** The palate is the foundation of the midface. A wider palate pushes the midface wider, which pushes the cheekbones wider. Wider zygos are one of the biggest halos in facial aesthetics. A narrow palate is one of the reasons some guys have flat, narrow midfaces no matter what they do.
- **Smile width.** Narrow palate = narrow dental arch = narrow smile. Wide palate = wider arch = broader, more attractive smile. This is one of the most noticeable differences.
- **Nasal airway.** The floor of your nasal cavity is the roof of your mouth. Wider palate = wider nasal floor = better airflow. Many guys who struggle with nasal breathing have narrow palates restricting their airway from below.
- **Ability to mew.** If your palate is too narrow, your tongue physically cannot fit flat against the roof of your mouth. The back third just has nowhere to go. You've been trying to mew for months and wondering why the tongue won't stay up? Your palate might be too narrow. Expand it first, then mew.
- **Tooth crowding.** The classic sign of a narrow palate. Teeth are crowded and overlapping because the arch is too narrow to fit them all. Many orthodontists "fix" this by extracting teeth and retracting the remaining ones, which actually makes the palate and midface even more recessed. That's the opposite of what you want.

[Image: image.png]

———

**Signs You Probably Need Expansion**

Look in the mirror. Open your mouth. Look at the roof of your mouth. Check these:
- **Crowded or overlapping teeth.** The number one indicator. If your teeth don't have room to sit straight in the arch, the arch is too narrow.
- **High, narrow arched palate.** If the roof of your mouth looks like a tall narrow dome rather than a wide shallow curve, it's narrow. A wide palate looks relatively flat and broad when you look up at it.
- **Crossbite.** Your upper teeth should sit slightly outside your lower teeth when you bite down. If upper teeth are inside the lowers on one or both sides, that's a crossbite from a narrow upper arch.
- **Chronic nasal congestion without obvious cause.** If ENTs say your anatomy is fine but you still can't breathe well through your nose, a narrow palate restricting the nasal floor could be the reason.
- **Tongue can't fit on the palate.** If you've been trying to mew and the back of your tongue just doesn't fit up there, this is probably why.
- **Narrow midface or flat cheekbones.** May be partially caused by a narrow palatal foundation.

If you have 2 or more of these, you should seriously look into expansion. And if you're under 16, the window is wide open.

———

**The Age Windows**

This is everything. Timing is the single biggest factor in how easy and how effective palate expansion is.

**Ages 10-14: The golden window.**

The midpalatal suture is fully open. Expansion with a simple RPE (rapid palate expander) is straightforward, well-studied, and produces permanent results. This is when orthodontists can widen your palate by 5-10mm with a device that takes about 3-6 months. The bone fills in, the palate stays wide, done. If you're in this age range and have any signs of a narrow palate, **this is the single highest ROI intervention in this entire course.**

**Ages 14-17: Still very doable.**

The suture is starting to interlock but is not fused. RPE or slow expansion still works for most guys in this range. Some resistance from the suture means it might take a bit longer or require slightly more force, but the outcomes are still excellent. Don't wait just because you're 15 and think you missed the window. You haven't.

**Ages 17-22: Harder but possible.**

The suture is partially or mostly fused in many guys by this age. Standard RPE becomes less predictable. This is where MSE (micro-implant-assisted expansion) comes in. MSE uses mini-screws anchored directly into the palatal bone to deliver the expansion force straight to the suture. It works in late teens and early 20s for many patients. More invasive than RPE but still non-surgical.

**Ages 22+: Surgical territory.**

The suture is fused. SARPE (surgically assisted rapid palate expansion) is the primary option. A surgeon makes a cut along the midpalatal suture to release it, then an expander widens it. More complex, more expensive, requires an oral surgeon working with an orthodontist. Still effective but a completely different level of intervention than what a 13 year old goes through.

**The takeaway: the younger you do this, the simpler, cheaper, and more effective it is. Every year you wait adds complexity.**

———

**The Devices**

**RPE (Rapid Palate Expander)**

The gold standard for adolescents. A metal framework that bonds to your upper back teeth with a screw mechanism in the middle. You (or a parent) turn the screw a small amount each day (usually a quarter turn, about 0.25mm). Over weeks, the palate gradually splits at the suture and new bone fills in the gap.
- **How long:** Active expansion phase is typically 2-4 weeks of daily turns, then the device stays in for 3-6 months to let the bone consolidate.
- **What it feels like:** Pressure in the midface area after each turn. Not painful but uncomfortable for 10-15 minutes. Some guys get a gap between their front teeth during expansion, which is actually a good sign because it means the suture is opening. The gap closes on its own or with braces afterward.
- **Results:** 5-10mm of palate widening. Measurable increase in nasal airway volume. Wider dental arch. Often followed by braces to align the teeth in the new wider arch.

**MSE (Micro-implant Supported Expansion)**

For older teens and young adults where the suture has started fusing. Similar concept to RPE but the force is delivered through 4 mini-screws placed into the palatal bone rather than through the teeth. This targets the suture directly.
- **Who it's for:** Typically 16-25 year olds. Some providers use it into the late 20s.
- **The screws:** Placed under local anesthesia. The screw placement itself takes about 20 minutes. Sounds worse than it is.
- **How long:** Similar timeline to RPE. Daily activations for 2-4 weeks, then several months of consolidation.

**SARPE (Surgically Assisted Rapid Palate Expansion)**

For adults with fully fused sutures. An oral surgeon makes a surgical cut along the midpalatal suture (and sometimes additional cuts in the lateral walls of the maxilla) to weaken the bone. Then an expander is placed and activated over the following weeks.
- **Who it's for:** Typically 22+ but depends on individual fusion status.
- **Recovery:** 1-2 weeks of swelling and discomfort. 3-6 months with the expander in place.
- **Cost:** Significantly more than RPE due to the surgical component. $5,000-$15,000+ depending on the surgeon and location.

———

**How To Talk To Your Orthodontist About This**

Most orthodontists are focused on straightening teeth, not optimizing facial aesthetics. You need to advocate for yourself. Here's exactly what to say and do:

**Step 1:** Schedule a consultation with an orthodontist. If you already have one, great. If not, ask your dentist for a referral, or Google "orthodontist palate expansion [your city]."

**Step 2:** At the appointment, say something like: *"I think my palate might be narrow. I have crowded teeth [or crossbite, or difficulty breathing through my nose]. Can you evaluate my palate width and tell me if expansion would be beneficial?"*

**Step 3:** They'll likely take impressions, X-rays, or a CBCT scan. These show the palate width and the status of the midpalatal suture. The data will tell you if expansion is indicated.

**Step 4:** If they recommend extractions and retraction instead of expansion, get a second opinion. Extractions narrow the arch, which can narrow the midface and make things worse aesthetically. Not all orthodontists think expansion-first. Find one who does. Some orthodontists specifically advertise growth-oriented or orthotropic approaches.

**Step 5:** If expansion is recommended, discuss the options (RPE, MSE) and timeline. Most treatment plans involve expansion first, then braces or aligners to straighten everything in the new wider arch.

**Key phrase to use:** *"I'd prefer to expand rather than extract."* This tells the orthodontist you're informed and you know there's more than one approach.

**Involve your parents.** If you're under 18, your parents are making the decisions and paying for treatment. Explain to them why you think expansion matters. Show them this module if you need to. The costs of palate expansion during adolescence (typically $2,000-$5,000 including the expander and follow-up braces) are a fraction of what adult SARPE costs, and the results are better.

———

**The Big Warning About Orthodontics**

I need you to understand this because it could save your face. Not all orthodontic treatment is equal from an aesthetic standpoint.

The traditional approach to crowding is: extract 4 premolars, then retract the remaining teeth inward with braces. This "solves" the crowding by making the arch smaller. But it makes the palate narrower, the midface flatter, and the profile more recessed. It literally does the opposite of what you want aesthetically.

**The expansion approach is: widen the palate first to make room, then align the teeth in the wider arch.** No extractions. No retraction. The palate gets wider, the midface gets wider support, the airway improves, and the teeth have room to sit properly.

If an orthodontist recommends extracting teeth for crowding without even discussing expansion, find another orthodontist. This is critical. The wrong orthodontic plan during puberty can permanently narrow your midface and worsen your facial aesthetics. Get informed. Ask questions. Get second opinions.

———

**Palate Expansion + Mewing**

These two go hand in hand and here's why. If your palate is too narrow for your tongue to rest flat on it, mewing is mechanically impossible. You can try all you want but the back third of your tongue just won't fit. Expansion gives the tongue room to sit on the palate properly. Once the palate is wide enough, mewing becomes natural and the tongue pressure helps maintain and even continue the expansion.

The ideal sequence for someone with a narrow palate during puberty:

1. Get palate expanded (RPE or MSE)
2. Begin proper mewing once the tongue has room
3. The tongue pressure helps maintain the new width and potentially contributes to further forward growth
4. Combined with nasal breathing and good posture, this is the full craniofacial optimization stack

———

If you're under 16 and you have any signs of a narrow palate (crowded teeth, high arch, crossbite, can't fit tongue on palate), getting an RPE is probably the single highest return intervention in this entire course. Simple device, a few months of treatment, permanent widening of your palate, cheekbones, nasal airway, and smile. It only gets harder and more expensive from here. Talk to an orthodontist. Ask about expansion. Involve your parents. Do it now while the suture is open and the window is wide.

That's the end of the puberty course. If you've read all 10 modules, you now know more about optimizing this developmental window than 99% of guys your age. Sleep, nutrition, breathing, oral posture, training, hormones, skin, posture, and palate expansion. Stack them all. Your future face, frame, and height are being decided right now. Make sure the decision goes in your favor.
