# Anti-Bloat Protocol

Source: "Facial Bloating: Why Your Face Looks Puffy & How To Fix It" + Expert analysis

## The 6 Science-Based Steps

1. **Understand the Main Cause** — For Mert: FODMAPs (edamame, corn, red cabbage, honey mango dressing) causing intestinal fermentation
2. **Increase Sodium's Counterplayer** — Potassium. Avocado is the primary potassium source in the bowls
3. **Manipulate the Lymphatic System** — Daily walking (10k steps from Day 8), lymphatic drainage massage from Day 7 post-surgery
4. **Avoid Late Night Carbs** — Last meal before 7 PM, no salad after 5 PM
5. **Fix Your Sleeping Position** — Head elevated 30–45° (also required post-surgery)
6. **Cold Exposure** — Cold shower or compress morning, ice packs face first 48h post-surgery

## Why Balsamic Vinegar (Not Other Dressings)
- Acetic acid reduces post-meal glucose spike (synergistic with semaglutide)
- Grape polyphenols (resveratrol analogs)
- Anti-bloat: acetic acid suppresses gas-producing bacteria
- 19 kcal vs 162 kcal for Caesar

## Daily Anti-Bloat Rules
- 3.5–4L water daily
- No alcohol (vasodilator, histamine trigger, disrupts sleep)
- No sodium-heavy processed food
- Salad at lunch, not dinner
- 10,000 steps from Day 8 (lymphatic pump)
- Lymphatic drainage massage from Day 7 (professional)

## For Scan Days (Tanita)
- Test first thing morning, post-toilet, before eating/drinking
- No salad within 3 hours of scan
- Balsamic on scan days: fine (doesn't dehydrate)
- Avocado on scan days: fine (counts as normal hydration food)
- 72h pre-scan depletion for "show number": 3L → 2.5L → 2L water, carbs 80 → 50 → 30g
