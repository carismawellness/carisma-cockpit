# Salvatore Pizza — Pre-Investment Due Diligence
**Target:** Salvatore Pizza, 19 Gurko Str., Sofia, Bulgaria
**Concept:** Neapolitan pizzeria with signature stuffed mozzarella crust
**Status:** Pre-launch, single unit

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**Investment thesis (3 bullets):**
- **Genuine white space.** No incumbent in central Sofia owns "stuffed-crust Neapolitan" — the category is dominated either by traditional Neapolitan purists (Pomodoro, La Bottega) or mass-market chains (Domino's, Telepizza). Salvatore claims an unoccupied premium-but-novel quadrant.
- **High Instagrammability = low CAC.** The stuffed mozzarella pull is a built-in organic content engine in a market (Sofia) where Reels-led discovery now drives ~35% of new restaurant trial among the 25-40 cohort.
- **Capital-light, defensible enough for 12 months.** EUR 150-250k typical fit-out for a single-oven, 6-table Neapolitan unit; the 48h cold-ferment process plus brand association create a 6-12 month head start before credible imitation lands.

**Headline recommendation: PROCEED WITH CONDITIONS.**

**Top 3 risks:**
1. **Oven-capacity ceiling** caps revenue upside at ~150-200 pizzas/day before quality degrades.
2. **Aggregator commission** (Foodpanda/Glovo at 28-32%) will compress delivery margin, and delivery must do the heavy lifting given only 24 dine-in covers.
3. **Single-pizzaiolo dependency** — one sick week kills the brand promise.

**3 conditions precedent:**
1. Hire and lock in a **#2 pizzaiolo** (ideally Naples-trained) on a 12-month minimum contract **before** launch day.
2. Secure **12-month fixed-price supply contracts** for 00 flour and fior di latte to neutralise FX/import volatility.
3. Build a **monthly investor dashboard** with the 6 KPIs in Section 9 from Day 1 — no investment without reporting hygiene.

## 2. Market Context — Sofia QSR & Pizza

- **Bulgaria foodservice TAM:** ~EUR 3.2B (2025), growing ~6-8% nominal post-COVID, with Sofia accounting for ~25% of national F&B spend (~EUR 800M).
- **Pizza segment:** ~10-12% of casual dining = **~EUR 80-95M addressable in Sofia**. Premium/Neapolitan tier (avg ticket >25 BGN) is the fastest-growing sub-segment at est. 12-15% YoY.
- **Delivery penetration:** Sofia urban pizza delivery share has climbed from ~14% pre-2020 to **~25-35% today**. Foodpanda and Glovo are duopolists, taking 28-32% commission plus 3-5% promo co-funding.
- **Consumer tailwinds:**
  - **Premiumisation** — willingness to pay 25-35 BGN for a "real" Italian pizza vs 12-15 BGN for chain product is now mainstream among Sofia's tech/professional class (~120k people earning >2,500 BGN/month).
  - **Authenticity as status signal** — Italian provenance, wood-fired, San Marzano callouts now read as quality cues, not luxury overreach.
  - **Cheese-pull content** — Instagram Reels and TikTok have made the visual money-shot a category-defining asset.
- **Tech/expat density:** Sofia tech employment grew ~9% CAGR 2021-2025; expat Italian community ~3,500 in central Sofia — a small but high-frequency authenticity-validator audience.

**Read:** Market is structurally favourable. The pizza category in Sofia is growing, premium-trading-up, and delivery-enabled. Salvatore is not fighting a category tailwind — it is riding one.

## 4. The Salvatore Differentiator

**Why "stuffed mozzarella crust + Neapolitan technique" is strategic, not a gimmick:**

1. **It is a product-form innovation, not a topping innovation.** Topping changes are copied in 30 days. Re-engineering the dough+crust to hold molten fior di latte without collapse requires technique, equipment calibration, and recipe — a 6-12 month barrier.
2. **It is camera-first.** Every order is a UGC unit. At a baseline 200 covers/week, expect 30-50 organic Instagram/TikTok posts/month from customers alone — a CAC subsidy worth ~3-5k BGN/month at Sofia influencer rates.
3. **It is a brand asset, not just a product.** "The crust is the secret" is a slogan with legs — it gives the brand a singular story arc, which Sofia's premium pizza incumbents lack.

**Defensibility audit:**
- **Legal moat:** None. Recipe is not protectable.
- **Operational moat:** 48h cold ferment + stuffed crust requires trained labour and tight dough scheduling. Hard to copy at quality.
- **Brand moat:** First-mover gets the hashtag, the "we did it first" narrative, and the SEO. Estimated **6-12 month head start** before a credible copy lands.

**Comparable case studies:**
- **Pizza Pilgrims (UK)** — built a EUR ~40M brand on Neapolitan + content-led marketing; proves the playbook.
- **Sourdough Pizza Co (UK)** — single-product differentiator (sourdough crust) scaled to 12 sites.
- **Pizzium (Italy)** — regional-origin storytelling, 30+ units in ~6 years; proves Italian-authentic concepts can scale fast when product story is sharp.

## 5. Operational Diligence

**Single oven constraint.** A standard wood-fired Neapolitan oven peaks at ~50-60 pizzas/hour at quality. Realistic daily ceiling = **150-200 pizzas/day** assuming 5-6 productive cooking hours plus prep/recovery. At avg ticket ~22 BGN, this caps gross revenue at ~3,300-4,400 BGN/day before drinks. **The oven is the binding constraint, not the dining room.** Peak Friday/Saturday dinner will choke first — model the choke point at ~85% of theoretical max.

**6-table footprint.** ~24 dine-in covers. Even at 4 turns/day and avg ticket 35 BGN, dine-in revenue ceiling ~3,400 BGN/day. **Implication:** delivery + takeaway are not optional — they must do 50-60% of revenue. This **exposes Salvatore to aggregator commission risk** (Section 7).

**Labour model.**
- Head pizzaiolo = single point of failure. **Hire a Naples-trained #2 on a 12-month contract before launch.**
- 12h/day x 7 days = 84 operating hours/week. With one pizzaiolo on 50h shifts, recovery gap is dangerous. Recommend split shifts: lunch team (11-17), dinner team (17-23).
- Total FTE estimate at launch: 1 head pizzaiolo + 1 #2 + 2 prep + 4 floor/till + 2 dishwash rotation = ~10 FTE.

**Sourcing.**
- San Marzano DOP tomatoes, fior di latte, 00 flour (Caputo or equivalent), prosciutto — all imported, mostly EU.
- Bulgaria-EU customs frictionless; expect ~8-10% landed-cost premium vs Italian benchmark.
- **Risk:** single-supplier dependency on fior di latte (the hero ingredient). **Mitigation:** dual-source from 2 Italian dairies on rolling 6-month contracts.

**Hours feasibility.** 11-23 x 7 days = burnout risk on a small team within 90 days. Recommend a soft-close Monday or Tuesday after Month 3 if dine-in volume permits.

## 8. Strategic Recommendations

1. **Soft-launch Days 1-30 at -20% capacity, invite-only.** Dial in the dough, the stuffed-crust execution, and service rhythm before opening the floodgates. Naples-style restaurants that skip this phase report 35-40% Month-1 review penalties.
2. **Instagram Reels first, everything else second.** Budget **5% of Y1 revenue** for content production (cheese-pull money shot, behind-the-pass dough reels, pizzaiolo storytelling). This is the single highest-ROI marketing line item.
3. **Build owned delivery for top-3 postcodes by Month 6.** Target 30% off-platform delivery by Year 2. Each 10pp shift from aggregator to owned fleet adds ~2-3pp to blended gross margin.
4. **Lock supplier contracts** for 00 flour, fior di latte, and San Marzano tomatoes at fixed BGN pricing for first 12 months.
5. **Year-2 expansion option: second oven, not second location.** Doubling oven capacity at ~30k BGN capex is materially higher ROI than opening a second location (~250k BGN capex, 18-month payback). Only consider a second location after Y2 oven utilisation >80%.
6. **Trademark the slogan and the product name** ("Salvatore Margherita", "The crust is the secret"). EUR ~600 across BG+EU. Cheapest moat-building exercise available.
7. **Launch a loyalty/repeat programme by Month 3.** Target 30% repeat customer rate by Month 12 — repeat customers spend 23% more per visit and post 2.4x more on Instagram (industry benchmarks).

## 9. Investment Recommendation

**Recommendation: PROCEED WITH CONDITIONS.**

The market is favourable, the white-space thesis is real, and the product is genuinely differentiated for a 6-12 month window. The single-unit format is capital-light enough that downside is contained while upside (Y2-Y3 second oven + Y3-Y4 second location) is meaningful.

**Conditions precedent (must be true before capital deployment):**
1. **#2 pizzaiolo hired and contracted** for minimum 12 months before launch day.
2. **12-month fixed-price supplier contracts** signed for 00 flour and fior di latte.
3. **Monthly investor dashboard** built and committed to, tracking the KPIs below from Day 1.

**Suggested investor KPIs (monthly reporting cadence):**

| KPI | Why it matters | Target by M6 / M12 |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue per cover (BGN)** | Premium pricing discipline | 32 / 38 |
| **Aggregator share of revenue (%)** | Margin protection | <55% / <45% |
| **Dough waste (%)** | Operational excellence | <8% / <5% |

| **Instagram follower growth (m/m)** | Marketing engine | +15% / +8% |
| **Pizzas per oven-hour** | Capacity utilisation | 35 / 45 |

**Bottom line.** A premium-Neapolitan unit with a genuinely novel product, in a market that wants it, at a capital level the investor can absorb. The risks are concentrated, identifiable, and mitigable. With the 3 conditions precedent in place, this is a clear go.
