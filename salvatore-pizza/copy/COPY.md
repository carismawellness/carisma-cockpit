## 5. "The crust is the secret" — signature section

- **Section H2:** The crust is the secret.

**Body:**
Most pizzerias treat the rim like a handle. We treat it like the main course. Every dough is mixed, rested, and cold-fermented for forty-eight hours until it carries a quiet, beery sweetness. We hand-stretch it, lay a ribbon of fior di latte along the edge, then fold and pinch the cornicione closed so the cheese is sealed inside before the pie ever sees the fire. Crushed San Marzano, torn buffalo mozzarella, fresh basil, a thread of Sicilian olive oil. Ninety seconds in a 480°C wood-fired oven and the base blisters into leopard spots while the hidden mozzarella turns molten. Lift a slice. The crust tears open. The cheese pulls.

**Feature bullets:**
- 48-hour cold-fermented Neapolitan dough
- Fior di latte sealed inside the rim
- 480°C wood fire, 90 seconds, blistered base

## 7. Our story (Salvatore)

- **H2:** Salvatore, and one stubborn idea.

Salvatore grew up in the back streets of Naples, in a kitchen where the men in his family argued about hydration percentages the way other families argue about football. He learned the rules. He kept most of them. The one rule he kept breaking was the cornicione — why, he asked, should the best part of the pizza be the part you leave on the plate? Years of stubborn experimentation gave him an answer: stretch the dough thicker at the rim, seal a ribbon of mozzarella inside, let the wood fire finish the trick. He brought the recipe to Sofia, set up at 19 Gurko, and let the pizza do the introducing.
