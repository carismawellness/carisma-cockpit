## 1. Brand Story

Salvatore learned to make pizza the way his grandfather did — hands in flour before sunrise, a wood-fired oven that crackled like a heartbeat, and the unshakeable belief that dough has a soul. He grew up in the back streets of Naples, watching the men of his family argue for hours about hydration percentages and the exact moment San Marzano tomatoes hit their peak. But Salvatore had one obsession the old men never approved of: he wanted the crust itself to be the surprise. Years of stubborn experimentation gave him the answer — a Neapolitan dough, fermented 48 hours, hand-stretched, then sealed around a ribbon of stretched mozzarella so the cornicione melts when you bite it. He brought the recipe to Sofia, set up at 19 Gurko, and decided the pizza would speak for itself. No theatre. Just dough, fire, and a crust that pulls.

## 2. Positioning Statement

For Sofia diners who refuse to compromise between authentic Italian tradition and crave-worthy indulgence, **Salvatore Pizza** is the Neapolitan pizzeria that turns the crust itself into the best bite of the meal, because every dough is hand-stretched, 48-hour fermented, and sealed with stretched mozzarella before it ever sees the fire.

## 3. Brand Personality

1. **Authentic** — Naples is not an aesthetic, it is a discipline. We follow the technique, not the trend.
2. **Confident** — We do one thing and we do it better than anyone else in Sofia. We do not over-explain it.
3. **Warm** — A pizzeria is a kitchen with a door open. Guests are family the moment they cross it.
4. **Sensory** — Everything we say, photograph, and serve must make you hungry within three seconds.
5. **Crafted** — Slow dough, careful hands, hot fire. We are makers, not assemblers.

## 4. Tone of Voice — 3 Rules

**Rule 1: Warm and Italian, never a caricature.**
- DO: "Forty-eight hours of patience. Ninety seconds in the fire. One unforgettable bite."
- DON'T: "Mamma mia! It'sa so good, capisce?!"

**Rule 2: Show the food, do not adjective-stack it.**
- DO: "The crust tears open. The mozzarella pulls."
- DON'T: "Our delicious, mouth-watering, amazing, premium gourmet pizza experience."

**Rule 3: Confident, not boastful. Let the technique speak.**
- DO: "Stuffed crust. Naples dough. No shortcuts."
- DON'T: "The best pizza in Sofia, voted #1 by everyone."

## 5. Tagline Options

1. **The crust is the secret.** ← PICK
2. Naples, sealed inside.
3. Hand-stretched. Cheese-sealed. Fire-finished.
4. A pizza you eat from the edge in.
5. Forty-eight hours. Ninety seconds. One bite.

**Why #1 wins:** Five words. Plants the differentiator (the crust) and creates intrigue (a secret). It works as a headline, a t-shirt, a window decal, a Reel hook, and a Google Ads line — without ever needing translation or set-up. The other taglines describe the product; #1 markets it.

## 6. Color System

| Token | Name | HEX | Use | Do NOT use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | **Salvatore Red** | `#C8102E` | Logo mark, primary CTA buttons, signature accents, awnings, pizza box, hero backgrounds | As large flat fills behind food photography (it competes with tomato red in the dish) |
| Anchor | **Deep Navy** | `#0E2A47` | Body text, headers on white, menu background option, packaging interior, formal collateral | As CTA color (reserved for red) |
| Base | **Pure White** | `#FFFFFF` | Primary canvas for menus, web, photography breathing room | For body text on red (use Off-White instead for warmth) |
| Base | **Off-White** | `#F7F1E3` | Backgrounds for menus, paper bags, secondary canvas, "tablecloth" feel | When clinical sharpness is needed — use Pure White |
| Accent | **Mozzarella Yellow** | `#F4C430` | Highlight strokes, "Stuffed Crust" callout badge, price flags, social motion graphics | As body text (poor contrast); never on Off-White |
| Neutral | **Charcoal** | `#1C1C1C` | Photography background, dark mode option, sub-headers when navy is too cool | When warmth is needed — use Navy instead |
| Neutral | **Crust Cream** | `#E8D8B7` | Texture backgrounds, dough/crust visual references, packaging trim | Anywhere it would muddy yellow — keep them separated |
| Neutral | **Charred Edge** | `#3A1F1A` | Wood-fire accent, photo overlays, gradient stops on red | As large flat areas (it reads as dirt at scale) |

**Hierarchy rule:** Red leads. Navy anchors. Yellow accents (never more than 10% of the canvas). White or Off-White carries the breathing room.

## 7. Typography Pairing (Google Fonts)

- **Display / Headlines / Logo lockup:** **Playfair Display** — high-contrast serif with editorial Italian-magazine feel. Used for the wordmark, hero headlines, menu category titles, and pull-quotes. Weight: 700 / 900 for impact, 400 italic for editorial captions.
- **Body / UI / Menu copy:** **Inter** — clean, modern, ultra-legible sans for menu items, descriptions, web body, prices, and signage. Weight: 400 for body, 500 for menu items, 700 for prices and CTAs.

This pairing gives editorial Italian heritage (Playfair) anchored by modern Sofia clarity (Inter) — exactly the bridge the brand needs.

## 8. Signature Visual Motifs

1. **The Cheese Pull.** Hero photography always captures the moment a slice is lifted and the mozzarella inside the crust stretches into a thread. This is the proprietary money shot. No other Sofia pizzeria can shoot it.
2. **The Charred Leopard Spots.** Macro photography of the underside and rim of the Neapolitan crust — the irregular blackened "leopardatura." Used as texture, background fills, and Reel transitions. Communicates true wood-fire authenticity instantly.
3. **The Sealed Cornicione Crescent.** A simple line illustration of a cross-section of the crust showing the stretched cheese sealed inside. Used as a brand icon, on packaging, as the favicon, and as a watermark over photography. This is Salvatore's "swoosh."

## 9. The Hero Product — "Salvatore Stuffed Crust Margherita"

A 30cm Neapolitan disc that begins with a 48-hour cold-fermented dough, hand-stretched until the rim — the cornicione — is thick enough to cradle a generous ribbon of fior di latte mozzarella. The edge is then folded, sealed, and pinched closed by hand before the pie is dressed with crushed San Marzano tomatoes, torn buffalo mozzarella, fresh basil, a thread of Sicilian olive oil, and a whisper of sea salt. Ninety seconds in a 480°C wood-fired oven and the base blisters into leopard spots while the hidden mozzarella inside the crust melts into a molten core. Pull a slice and the cheese pours from the rim. The first bite is the crust, and the crust is the surprise — soft, smoky, and stretching. That is the whole brand, in one mouthful.

## 11. Customer Personas

### 1. Nikolay, 32 — The Foodie Local
Works in tech near the city centre. Follows Sofia food bloggers on Instagram and has already eaten at every "best pizza in Sofia" candidate. Comes to Salvatore because the stuffed crust is a story he can post — the cheese pull is content. Returns because the dough is, quietly, the real reason.

### 2. Elena, 38 — The Family Organiser
Mother of two, lives in Lozenets, drives the family's "where are we eating tonight" decision. Wants somewhere that feels special enough for a Saturday evening but is fast and welcoming enough for a Wednesday with the kids. The stuffed crust is non-negotiable for her 9-year-old, the authentic Margherita is non-negotiable for her husband. Salvatore wins both.

### 3. Marco, 45 — The Expat Italian
Italian executive based in Sofia. Sceptical of any pizzeria outside Italy by default. Walks in once to test the dough, looks at the leopardatura, takes one bite, and becomes a once-a-week regular. He is the brand's ultimate word-of-mouth engine: when Marco tells his colleagues "this is the real thing," half the office books a table.
